# Affiliate Gap Filling — Money Model Without New Products

## Overview

Sell someone else's product for a commission when you don't have one for a specific stage of your money model. Zero product development. Zero delivery. Pure margin addition.

**Core principle:** You can always offer somebody else's stuff. You don't need to build a new business to make another offer.

---

## When to Use Affiliates

| Situation | What to fill |
|---|---|
| Starting out with one product | Upsell and continuity stages |
| No recurring product | Continuity stage |
| No physical product complement | Upsell stage (tools, equipment, supplements) |
| $100M business with operational limits | Revenue expansion without headcount |
| Niche with natural adjacencies | Any stage |

---

## How Affiliate Relationships Work

1. You sell the other company's product to your customers
2. They deliver it
3. They pay you a commission (typically 10–50% depending on industry)
4. You do no fulfillment work

**Two structures:**
- **Direct affiliate:** Company pays you per sale
- **White-label:** You sell their product under your brand, they fulfill

---

## Finding What to Affiliate

Map your customer journey. At each stage after your offer, ask:

> "What's the next problem my customer has after buying my thing?"

That next problem = potential affiliate product slot.

**Examples:**

| Your business | Your product | Affiliate upsell |
|---|---|---|
| Fitness coaching | 12-week program | Supplements, home equipment |
| Marketing agency | Ad campaigns | CRM software, landing page tool |
| Business course | Training content | Accounting software, legal services |
| Dental agency | Client acquisition | Equipment manufacturer, supplies |
| Newsletter | Written content | Books, courses, software they'll use |

---

## Scripts for Affiliate Offers

**Classic Upsell style:**
> "Most people who go through [your program] also need [affiliate product]. I've partnered with [company] — it's what I personally use / recommend to all my clients. Here's a link and you get [X% off / bonus] as a client of mine."

**Continuity style (tool they'll use ongoing):**
> "For the ongoing work, you'll want [software]. I have an affiliate deal — you get [discount/trial] and I get a small commission. Win-win."

---

## Curating Affiliates

Only affiliate products you actually believe in:
1. Use it yourself first if possible
2. Vet the company's customer service
3. Check their refund/dispute handling (your reputation is attached)
4. Negotiate: better commission, exclusive discount for your customers, co-marketing

A bad affiliate experience damages your relationship with the customer. High standards here.

---

## Revenue Impact

Even a modest affiliate addition can materially change economics:

**Example:** 100 customers/month, 30% take affiliate upsell at $200 product, 40% commission
= 30 sales × $200 × 0.4 = **$2,400/month, zero delivery work**

Over 12 months: $28,800 in pure-margin revenue from one affiliate relationship.

---

## Affiliate Gap-Filling by Money Model Stage

| Stage | What to affiliate |
|---|---|
| **Attraction** | Partner products that attract leads (free tools, lead magnets from complementary businesses) |
| **Upsell** | Natural next product after your core offer |
| **Downsell** | Cheaper alternative to your offer (rather than losing the customer entirely) |
| **Continuity** | Subscription tool / service they'll use alongside yours |

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Affiliating everything → trust erosion | Only promote what you'd stake your reputation on |
| No vetting of affiliate company | Test their customer service before sending clients |
| Keeping standard commission | Negotiate: your audience is an asset, use it |
| One-off affiliate push | Best affiliates are embedded in your standard sales flow |
