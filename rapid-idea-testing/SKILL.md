# Rapid Idea Testing

## Overview

Never build what you don't know people will buy. Every idea needs a test. The smaller, cheaper, and faster the test, the better. The goal is **signal, not proof** — a confirmed signal is worth a thousand plans.

**Core sequence:** Get flow → Monetize flow → Add friction

Ungate first to get attention. Then see if people pay. Then add qualification and scale.

---

## Step 1: Define the Hypothesis

One sentence: "I believe [specific person] will pay [price] for [outcome] because [reason]."

- Specific person: not "entrepreneurs" — "gym owners doing $10k/mo with at least one employee"
- Specific outcome: not "better results" — "10 new clients in 30 days"
- Named price to test

**Red flag:** If you can't write this sentence, the idea isn't ready to test. Sharpen it first.

---

## Step 2: Choose a Test Wrapper

| Wrapper | When to use | What it tests |
|---|---|---|
| **Free** | No audience yet, no proof | Attention — will people raise their hand? |
| **Discounted / Early Access** | Small warm audience, some credibility | Willingness to pay something |
| **Full Price** | Existing audience, proven delivery | Real demand at market price |

**Hormozi's Law:** Free gets 9× more takers than $0.01. If you need speed and have no existing customers, start free. But free proves interest — not willingness to pay. Test paying soon after.

---

## Step 3: Identify Your Warmest Audience

Test in this order (warmest → coldest):

1. **Existing customers** — already trust you, lowest friction
2. **Past customers** — know your value, may need re-activation
3. **Engaged leads** — warm, opted in
4. **Cold audience** — only after validating with warm

Warm audience tests take 7 days or less. Cold tests take months and cost money. Always start warm.

---

## Step 4: Run the Minimum Viable Test

**7-day maximum.** Longer = campaign, not test.

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Day 7 | Tease: "Something big coming tomorrow" (text + email) |
| Day 6 | Announce: Core offer, 1-3 components, price range |
| Day 3 | Update: How many sold, how many spots left |
| Day 2 | Warning: Spots running out + add a bonus |
| Day 1 AM | Last call: Drop best bonus here |
| Day 0 | Close: Sold out announcement |

**For micro-tests (< 10 target buyers):** Skip the sequence entirely. Make a direct personal offer to 5-10 people who fit the hypothesis. A direct yes or no is more valuable than a sequence with silence.

---

## Step 5: Measure the Signal

Track these four in order:

| Metric | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **Response rate** | Does your audience care about the topic? |
| **Conversion rate** | Will they pay? Is the offer compelling? |
| **Fulfillment satisfaction** | Can you actually deliver the outcome? |
| **Testimonial / repeat buy** | Is the result real? Do they want more? |

**Success threshold:** 1 in 10 warm-audience contacts buys = idea has legs. 0 in 10 = diagnose before pivoting.

---

## Step 6: Interpret and Decide

| Result | Diagnosis | Next step |
|---|---|---|
| Nobody responded | Wrong audience or wrong topic | Change who you're talking to |
| Responses but no buys | Interest exists, offer/price/trust is off | Improve offer, lower price, or add proof |
| Buys but terrible fulfillment | Idea works, execution broken | Fix delivery before scaling |
| Buys + happy customers | Signal confirmed | Run full offer, raise price |

**Nudge-up rule:** Once selling, raise price 20% every 10 sales until conversion drops dramatically. Go back one step. That's your sweet spot.

---

## Common Testing Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Testing cold audience first | Always start warm |
| Running a 30-day test | Cap at 7 days — urgency drives decisions |
| Measuring vanity metrics (likes, opens) | Measure buys and fulfillment satisfaction only |
| Building before testing | One sale beats one thousand plans |
| Concluding "no demand" from "no audience" | Under-50 warm contacts = inconclusive, not negative |
| Testing a complex version | Test the simplest path to the core outcome first |
