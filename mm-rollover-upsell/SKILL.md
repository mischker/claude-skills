# Rollover Upsell — Upsell Offer

## Overview

Credit some or all of a customer's previous purchase toward a new, more expensive offer. Instead of giving a refund, you roll the money forward. Customers feel like they're getting their money back while actually spending more.

**Core mechanic:** People don't want to lose money they already spent. Rolling it forward feels like winning — and gets them into a bigger commitment.

## Four Situations to Use Rollover Upsells

| Who | Situation | Script opening |
|---|---|---|
| **Old customers** (6+ months silent) | Win-back campaign | "I want to give you your money back as credit..." |
| **Upset customers** | Rescue before refund | "I want to make this right — here's what I'll do..." |
| **Competitors' upset customers** | Steal from bad reviews | "I saw your review. I'll credit what you're owed..." |
| **Current customers** | After short-term purchase | "Let me apply what you just paid toward the full year..." |

## Pricing Rule

Make the upsell offer at least **4x more expensive than the rollover credit**.
- If you roll over $600, the upsell should be $2,400+
- This gives a 25% effective discount while preserving margin
- The bigger the upsell, the smaller the credit's impact on margin

## How to Apply the Credit

| Method | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Upfront (full credit)** | Old customers / re-engagement; high impact needed |
| **Spread over term** | Current customers on payment plans ($600 credit = $50/mo off for 12 months) |
| **Partial credit** | When margin is tight; test what amount triggers commitment |

## Win-Back Campaign Script (Old Customers)

> "Hi [Name], I want to give you some of your money back — do you have a minute? I've been thinking about your [past result]. I want to offer you $[credit] toward [new offer] as a thank you for being a past customer. This would get you [specific benefit]. Want to hear more?"

Hormozi sent 200 personalized videos offering $4,000 in credit → 20% conversion → ~$1.9M additional annual revenue.

## Competitor's Customer Script

> "Hi [Name], I saw your review of [competitor]. I'm genuinely frustrated by that — I'd love to make it up to you. I'll credit whatever you have left with them toward a [term] agreement with us. You don't lose anything, and you start getting [benefit] today. Fair?"

Find leads via negative reviews on competitor products (scrape contact info where available).

## The Gift Card Play (Full Mechanics)

1. Create a referral gift card worth $200
2. Sell it to existing customers for $20 (90% off); limit 2 per customer per year (prevents arbitrage)
3. Tell them to give it to a friend — when the friend uses it, apply the $200 toward an offer with $1,000+ price tag
4. Track via unique code per card (not physical cards) so you know which customer referred whom
5. Unused cards expire after 24 months → pure revenue

**Result:** Customers pay you to generate referrals. Unused cards are profit. The $200 gift card has a 20% discount effect on a $1,000 offer — well within margin.

## Urgency Add-On

Make the rollover a one-time offer. If they don't take it now, the credit goes away.
> "I can only offer this rollover right now — it's a one-time option. If it makes sense for you, great. If not, no problem, you can always come back at full price."

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Rolling over 100% → no margin | Keep upsell 4x higher than credit |
| No urgency → they sleep on it | Make it one-time-only; now or never |
| Using rollover as first offer | Lead with full-price offer; rollover is for situations above |
| Offering refund before rollover | Always try rollover upsell before processing any refund |
