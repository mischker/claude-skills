# Anchor Upsell — Upsell Offer

## Overview

Present your most expensive option first. If the customer hesitates ("The Gasp"), offer a cheaper-but-still-good alternative. The expensive option anchors value; the main offer now feels like a deal.

**Core mechanic:** Context determines perception. A $2,200 suit feels cheap after a $16,000 suit. A $200/mo plan feels cheap after a $1,000/mo plan.

**Key insight:** Making a $100 offer to someone with a $1,000 budget loses you $900. You lose nothing by offering premium first — some will buy it.

**Relationship to Classic Upsell:** This is a perception-first variant of `mm-classic-upsell`. Use Classic Upsell when the next problem is clear and obvious. Use Anchor Upsell when you want to shift budget expectations before presenting your core offer.

## Steps

**1. Present the Anchor** (5–10x more expensive than main offer)
Sell it seriously. Don't gloss over it — if you treat it like a fake, so will the customer.

**2. Get The Gasp**
Expect the customer to hesitate or show shock. The bigger the gasp, the more they'll spend on the main offer.

When you get The Gasp: pause 3 seconds, stay calm, then ask — don't fill the silence with justifications.

**3. Come to the Rescue**
Ask what makes the premium version premium, then check if they care about that.
> "Do you care about [premium differentiator]?" / "Is [designer / concierge / speed] important to you?"

**4. Present the Main Offer**
Only differ on secondary features (designer, material, response time, level of service). Keep core features the same.
> "If not, this one covers everything you actually need for [1/5th the price]."

**5. Ask for Payment**
> "Which card do you want to use?"

## Design the Anchor Correctly

- Premium must have the **same primary features** as main offer
- Premium differs on **secondary features** (material, designer, SLA, access, speed)
- Difference in price: 5–10x minimum
- Make a premium offer you'd actually be happy to deliver — some customers buy it

## Examples

| Business | Premium (Anchor) | Main Offer |
|---|---|---|
| Consulting | Done-for-you, CEO access, daily Slack | Done-with-you, weekly calls |
| Newsletter | All archives + early access | Current issues only |
| Lawn care | Personal cell, fancy mulch, bi-weekly | Team number, standard mulch |
| Coaching | 1:1 daily coaching, unlimited calls | Group coaching, 2 calls/mo |

## Two Bonuses of Anchor Upsells

1. **Anchored customers spend more** than they planned — even if they take the main offer
2. **Some customers actually buy the premium** — pure profit with minimal extra work

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Glossing over the premium → no anchor | Present premium seriously; sell it like you want them to buy it |
| Premium and main offer are identical | Remove only secondary features; keep core the same |
| Gasping when they say yes to premium | Stay calm — you made a big sale |
| No premium offer at all | Always have a premium tier; you're leaving money on the table |
