# Offer Stacking — Creative Money Model Combinations

## Overview

The four offer types (Attraction, Upsell, Downsell, Continuity) are building blocks, not a rigid sequence. Once you understand each, combine them to build more powerful offers than any single model alone.

**Core principle:** More ways to offer the same product, not more products to offer.

---

## The Standard Sequence vs. Creative Stacking

**Standard (learn this first):**
Attraction → Upsell → Downsell → Continuity

**Creative (once standard is mastered):**
- Continuity as Attraction
- Upsell tactics inside Attraction
- Downsell inside Continuity
- Multi-headed offers combining 4–6 tactics at once

---

## Patterns

### 1. Continuity as Attraction Offer
Use a low monthly price to attract more customers than a high one-time price. Works when you have a strong recurring product.

**Trade-off:** Fewer dollars today, more over time. Only works if your 30-day unit economics allow it (other revenue sources cover CAC).

**Example:** SaaS free tier → paid monthly → annual prepay upsell

### 2. Free Trial + Pay Less Now + Lifetime Discount (The 6-Headed Monster)
Newsletter example from the book:
- Free trial (attraction)
- $399/mo after 30 days (continuity)
- "Pay $297 now and keep that rate for life" (Pay Less Now + Lifetime Discount + Prepay Upsell)

Single offer combines: Free Trial, Pay Less Now, Pay More Later, Lifetime Discount, Continuity, and Upsell. One sentence. Huge conversion.

### 3. Upsell Tactics Inside Attraction
Use Menu Upsell or Anchor Upsell mechanics during the initial sale, not just after.

**Example:** Present premium package first (Anchor), then standard as the "deal." Customer hasn't bought yet — this is still the attraction offer.

### 4. Buy X Get Y Free + Continuity Auto-Renewal
Turn a one-time bulk buy into continuity:
> "Buy 6 months, get 2 months free. After your 8 months, continues month-to-month at $[rate]. Cancel anytime."

Two-for-one: attraction offer and continuity in one sentence.

### 5. Win Your Money Back + Rollover Upsell
After customer wins money back (attraction), roll it into a longer continuity commitment instead of refunding cash:
> "You earned your $600 back. Want me to apply that as $50/month off a 12-month membership instead?"

They feel like they won; you turn a refund into continuity.

### 6. Giveaway + Pay Less Now (Non-Winner Close)
After giveaway, offer non-winners the Pay Less Now structure:
> "You didn't win the grand prize, but for 7 days you can get [full thing] at [discounted price] — or we can do [conditional pricing] if you'd like to try first."

### 7. Feature Downsell Inside Continuity
When a continuity customer wants to cancel, Feature Downsell them:
> "Instead of canceling, what if we cut [feature] and dropped your rate to $[lower]?"

Keeps them in the ecosystem at lower margin vs. losing them entirely.

## The Affiliate Gap Fill

If you don't have a product for a specific stage of your money model:
- Find a complementary business whose product fits that gap
- Set up an affiliate relationship (you sell, they deliver, you earn commission)
- Zero operational overhead; pure margin

**Example stages to fill with affiliates:**
- No upsell product? → Affiliate a tool your customers need next
- No continuity? → Affiliate a subscription service they'll use ongoing
- No physical product? → Affiliate merchandise or equipment

---

## Mix-and-Match Rules

1. **Master standard sequence first** — creative stacking requires understanding each piece
2. **One combination at a time** — test one creative stack, measure results, then iterate
3. **The customer's experience must be coherent** — creative stacks that confuse customers hurt conversion
4. **Maintain 30-day profit target** — any combination must still make CAC + COGS in 30 days
5. **Simple scales; fancy fails** — the most elegant stacks are 2–3 mechanics, not 6

---

## Checklist Before Stacking

```
[ ] Standard sequence already tested and working?
[ ] New combination solves a real constraint (more volume? more cash? lower churn?)
[ ] Customer journey is still coherent and easy to follow?
[ ] 30-day unit economics still positive with the new structure?
[ ] One variable changed at a time?
```
