# First Avatar — Vista PE Method

## Overview

Most businesses target broadly and serve whoever shows up. The Vista PE method does the opposite: find the 20% of customers who generate 80% of revenue, identify their common traits, and eliminate everyone else.

**The math:** Top customers may be worth 6–8x more than average. Gym Launch's top customers had $42K first-year LTV vs. $5K for competitor's customers. Same ads, same market — different targeting.

**The principle:** Quality > quantity. Removing qualification steps increases leads but decreases money.

---

## 4-Step Process

### Step 1: Survey and score your customers

Pull all customer data. Score every customer:
- How much have they paid total?
- How long have they stayed?
- How often do they engage / use the product?
- Have they referred others?

Calculate a composite score: **total gross profit × retention length**. This is the LTV score.

### Step 2: Find your top 20%

Take the top 20% by LTV score. These are your ideal customers.

If you don't have enough data, use the 3-month-or-longer retention filter as a proxy.

### Step 3: Find the common factors

Analyze everything about that top 20%:

**Demographics:** age, gender, income, geography, job title, company size, industry

**Psychographics:** mindset, values, beliefs, goals, fears, aspirations

**Buying behavior:**
- How did they find you?
- What channel?
- What content did they consume before buying? (Hormozi's data: 78% consumed 2+ long-form pieces before buying)
- What was the trigger event that made them buy now?
- What objections did they raise vs. not raise?
- How long was their sales cycle?

**Situation:** What were they doing before? What problem were they actively trying to solve?

Look for 3–5 factors that appear across all top customers. These become your avatar qualifiers.

**Survey questions to add to your intake:**
- What's your biggest challenge with [problem area] right now?
- What have you tried before? What didn't work?
- What's your goal in the next 6–12 months?
- What almost stopped you from signing up?
- How did you hear about us?

### Step 4: Execute — rewrite and re-engineer

**Rewrite your ads** to speak only to the qualifying factors you found. Use their language, their situation, their aspirations. Your ads should feel invisible to anyone outside the avatar and magnetic to anyone inside it.

**Re-engineer your sales process** to:
1. Filter out non-avatars early (intake questions, qualification calls)
2. Replicate the journey your best customers took — same content touchpoints, same objection sequence, same decision trigger
3. Price in a way that attracts your best customers and repels your worst (raising prices often self-selects for better customers)

---

## Example

Gym Launch's top customers were: conservative married male gym owners, age 25–45, with $10K+/month revenue. Once identified:
- Ads were rewritten to speak to married male gym owners explicitly
- Sales process was redesigned to qualify for those criteria
- Revenue per customer: $42K vs. $5–8K industry average
- Result: 70x more profit than competitor spending similar ad budgets

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Skipping this because you think you know your customer | You probably think your customer is "everyone who has the problem." Run the analysis — you'll find 3-5 specific qualifiers you didn't know about |
| Using only demographic data | Psychographics and buying behavior are more predictive. Add them |
| Not updating your messaging after finding the avatar | The analysis means nothing if the ads still say "for everyone" |
| Removing qualification steps to get more leads | More leads ≠ more money. Better-qualified leads = more money |
| Running this once | Revisit every 6–12 months as your business evolves |
