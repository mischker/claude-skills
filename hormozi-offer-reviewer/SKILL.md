# Hormozi Offer Reviewer

## Overview

Any offer can be scored against the Value Equation and ten structural elements. Weak conversion almost always traces to one of them. Run through this checklist top to bottom — each section builds on the previous one.

**Value Equation:** Value = (Dream Outcome × Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

**Review order:** Outcome clarity → Proof → Friction reduction → Structure → Naming

---

## 1. Dream Outcome Check

Does the copy sell the destination, or describe the plane ride?

- [ ] Is there a specific, named outcome (not "better results", not "growth")?
- [ ] Is it time-bound ("in 6 weeks", "by day 30")?
- [ ] Is it tied to buyer's status or identity ("gym owner making $1M", "CEO who doesn't work weekends")?
- [ ] Is it stated in the buyer's words, not the seller's jargon?

**Red flags:** "Comprehensive solution", "unlock your potential", "transform your business" — all vague. Replace with the specific before/after the buyer experiences.

---

## 2. Likelihood / Proof Check

Does the buyer believe this will work *for them specifically*?

- [ ] Is there social proof (testimonials, case studies, customer count)?
- [ ] Is proof specific (names, numbers, before/after data, timeframes)?
- [ ] Does proof feature buyers similar to the current reader?
- [ ] Is the mechanism explained (why it works, not just that it works)?
- [ ] Is there a guarantee that removes purchase risk?

**Hormozi's rule:** Proof that mirrors the buyer's exact situation is the strongest trust builder. Generic testimonials = weak. Named, specific, same-avatar testimonials = strong.

---

## 3. Time & Effort Reduction Check

Does the buyer believe this will be fast and easy enough to be worth trying?

- [ ] Is there a quick win promised early in the experience?
- [ ] Is setup described as simple or handled for them?
- [ ] Are DFY (done-for-you) or DWY (done-with-you) elements called out?
- [ ] Are templates, checklists, or pre-built assets mentioned?
- [ ] Is "how long until I see results" explicitly answered?

**Hormozi's rule:** Fast beats free. People pay a premium for speed. If your offer is slower than alternatives, you must justify the tradeoff or it kills conversion.

---

## 4. Problem Coverage Check

Are all major objections pre-answered in the copy?

Run through the 4 objection dimensions — mark any unaddressed:

- [ ] **Financial worth**: Is ROI or value-versus-cost made explicit?
- [ ] **Likelihood ("won't work for me")**: Is there a "even if..." counter?
- [ ] **Effort ("too hard/complicated")**: Is simplicity or support demonstrated?
- [ ] **Time ("too busy")**: Is the time commitment made small and clear?

Every unaddressed objection = a potential buyer who clicked away silently.

---

## 5. Guarantee / Risk Reversal Check

Is the buyer's risk fully reversed?

- [ ] Is there any guarantee at all?
- [ ] Is it conditional (requires buyer action) or unconditional (no questions asked)?
- [ ] Is the guarantee named and framed as a benefit?
- [ ] Is it tied to the dream outcome ("if you don't get X in Y days, we will Z")?
- [ ] Would doubling the guarantee strength increase conversion more than refunds?

**Hormozi's math:** If conversion rises 30% and refunds double (5%→10%), you still net +23% profit. Weak guarantees cost more than they save.

---

## 6. Scarcity & Urgency Check

Is there a real reason to act now?

- [ ] Is there a deadline (real, not fake)?
- [ ] Is there a quantity limit (real, not manufactured)?
- [ ] Is either justified with a real reason?
- [ ] Is "sold out" social proof used from prior rounds?

**Hormozi's data:** 50-60% of sales happen in the last 4 hours of a deadline. Without urgency, buyers delay indefinitely. Fake urgency destroys trust — use real constraints only.

---

## 7. Bonus Stack Check

Is the offer broken into named components, each addressing a specific obstacle?

- [ ] Is the core offer supplemented by bonuses?
- [ ] Does each bonus have a benefit-driven name?
- [ ] Does each bonus have an assigned dollar value?
- [ ] Does each bonus address a distinct obstacle from the problem list?
- [ ] Does total perceived value of all components exceed the price?

**Hormozi's rule:** A single offer is less valuable than the same offer broken into named parts. Each component named and valued separately gets credit it wouldn't get as part of a monolith.

---

## 8. Naming Check (M-A-G-I-C)

Apply to the overall offer name and to each bonus:

| Letter | Present? | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **M** — Magnetic reason | [ ] | "Zero-config", "Automated", "Instant" |
| **A** — Avatar (specific) | [ ] | "For gym owners", "For B2B agencies" |
| **G** — Goal (outcome) | [ ] | "Double revenue", "Pain-free close" |
| **I** — Interval (timeframe) | [ ] | "In 21 days", "By week 3" |
| **C** — Container word | [ ] | "Blueprint", "System", "Accelerator" |

Bad: "Marketing Package v2" → Good: "6-Week Revenue Accelerator for Gym Owners"

---

## 9. Pricing Model Check

Is the price justified by value, or anchored to cost/competitors?

- [ ] Is there an anchor price (original/retail value) shown before the offer price?
- [ ] Is the price positioned as an investment with ROI, not a cost?
- [ ] Is there an ultra-premium tier available (10x the 10% rule)?
- [ ] Is value-based pricing used (price = perceived outcome value), not cost-plus or competitor-match?

**Hormozi's finding:** Improving pricing by 1% is 4× more profitable than improving acquisition by 1%. Under-pricing is the most common and most expensive mistake.

---

## 10. Avatar Specificity Check

Does the copy qualify good-fit buyers in and repel bad-fit buyers out?

- [ ] Is there a named, specific avatar?
- [ ] Are minimum requirements stated explicitly?
- [ ] Does the copy use the avatar's exact language (problems, aspirations, vocabulary)?
- [ ] Would a bad-fit buyer self-select out after reading?

**Hormozi's principle:** Generic copy attracts generic buyers. Specific copy attracts ideal buyers who stay longer, pay more, and refer others.

---

## Fix Order

Count unchecked boxes per section, then fix in this sequence:

**Dream Outcome → Proof/Guarantee → Scarcity/Urgency → Bonuses → Naming**

Never fix naming before the outcome is clear. Never fix bonuses before the guarantee exists. Fix structural weaknesses before cosmetic ones.
