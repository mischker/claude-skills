# Trial With Penalty — Downsell Offer

## Overview

Customers start for free. They get it free so long as they do specific actions (attend calls, complete work, hit milestones). If they miss the actions, they pay a fee. This creates accountability, filters serious customers, and turns free trials into paying customers.

**Core mechanic:** Avoiding a fee is more motivating than earning a reward. Customers who do the required actions become great long-term customers.

**When to use:** After Payment Plan Downsell fails. For recurring products/services where the customer must do work to get results.

## How to Structure It

**What they get for free:** Your core offer (the same criteria as Win Your Money Back, ideally)

**What they must do to avoid the fee:**
- Attend all check-in calls (which become your upsell opportunities)
- Complete required homework/actions
- Show up to mandatory milestones

**The fee:** Either one lump sum at violation, or a smaller fee per missed item. Breaking fees up by criterion tends to keep people engaged and feel fairer.

## The 5-Step Downsell Process

**Step 1 — Offer the trial last**
Only after other downsells fail:
> "You know what — how about we just get you started for free? We'll help you out, and if you like it you can stay. What card do you want to use?"

**Step 2 — Always get a card**
No card = no trial. Non-negotiable.
> "That's just how we've always done it." (If they refuse, wish them well and move on — non-card customers complain more and convert less.)

**Step 3 — Sell staying and paying**
> "If this program gets you the result, will you stay long-term?" They must say yes. If no → no point in giving a trial.

**Step 4 — Explain the fees AFTER getting the card**
> "We'll do our part so long as you do yours. If you miss anything, you get a small fee — it keeps you on track. If you follow through, you get all this for free. Best of both worlds."

Note: Explaining fees before the card increases resistance. Explain after, with "that's just how we've always done it" energy. Have customers initial next to fee clauses.

**Step 5 — Explain check-ins (your upsell meetings)**
> "You agree to three check-in calls. First one we do [X] so you can [benefit]. Second [Y] for [benefit]. Third [Z] for [benefit]. Missing them triggers a fee because that's where we get you results."

## What To Do at the End of Trial

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| **They like it** | Already on autopay — still meet; offer longer term or premium |
| **They hate it** | Ask what would've been different; pitch a higher-level offer (works ~50% of the time) |
| **Didn't use it** | Reach out repeatedly before billing; offer to waive fee if they come in; use meeting to re-engage or upsell |

## Important Notes

- Just call it a "Free Trial" — don't say "Trial With Penalty" (sounds scary)
- Mirror the action criteria from Win Your Money Back offer — same behaviors that make great long-term customers
- First $1 is better than free: "First month for $1 then $[X]" — validates payment method
- Tweak if broken: low take rate → lower requirements; low conversion after trial → improve upsell meetings

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| No card required → no leverage | Card is non-negotiable; no card = no trial |
| Fees explained upfront → resistance | Get card first, explain fees after |
| No mandatory meetings → no upsells | Make check-ins required as part of fee-avoidance terms |
| Billing non-starters → bad reviews | Reach out multiple times; waive fees; don't nuke a relationship for a small fee |
