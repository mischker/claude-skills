# Cold Outreach — Get Leads From Strangers

## Overview

Cold outreach is one-to-one private communication with people who don't know you. Unlike warm outreach (limited by your existing contacts) or paid ads (limited by budget), cold outreach lets you pick your exact target audience and reach them at scale.

**Key insight:** Cold outreach is a numbers game that compounds. Once you figure out the inputs-to-outputs equation (X contacts = Y customers), you just need to do more.

**Cold vs. warm:** Strangers give you far less time to prove your worth. They don't trust you. This creates 3 problems to solve in order:
1. Getting their contact information
2. Getting them to pay attention
3. Getting them interested

---

## Step 1: Build the List

You get to pick exactly who you reach out to. "Only want to talk to hedge fund managers over $1B? Done."

**Three methods (in order):**

**Software:** Subscribe to lead scraping tools (search "outbound leads scraping tool"). Filter by your criteria (job title, company size, location, industry). Test a sample of 200–300 before buying in bulk.

**Brokers:** Pay list brokers to assemble targeted lists. Test sample lists from multiple brokers before committing. Contact information tends to be better quality.

**Manual (elbow grease):** Join communities, groups, or directories where your audience hangs out. When you find people that meet your criteria, extract their contact info or reach out within the platform. These leads are the freshest — less likely to have received cold outreach from others — but take the most time.

**Work in that order.** If you can search a database, so can your competitors. Manually assembled lists are the freshest and least burned.

**Target:** Start with 1,000 names. That gives you ~10 days of work (100 contacts/day) plus follow-up time.

## Step 2: Know What to Say

Two things must happen in the first 30 seconds: **personalization** (they feel like you know them) and **big fast value** (they feel stupid saying no).

### A. Personalization — Act Like You Know Them

The goal: make your cold message feel like a warm one.

Find 1–3 specific things about the person:
- Recent post they wrote
- A business milestone they hit
- Something about their company or industry

Use it in your opening line before anything else:
> "Hi [Name] — I watched your recent video on client retention and that point about onboarding changed how I think about it. Quick question…"

**Cold call example:**
> "Hey [Name]? …[pause] I watched a few of your videos and read your blog post on dog training. The peanut butter trick really worked on my doberman. Thanks for that. Anyway, I work with dog trainers to fill up their appointment books. We helped someone in your area get 100 new appointments in 30 days. If you got a boatload of new clients, you wouldn't be upset with me, would you? …Great — I can walk you through the whole thing today. Are you around at 4?"

The goal isn't to trick them — it's to treat them like a person. Take 2 minutes to learn something real about them before reaching out.

**Action: Batch research.** Spend one block of time researching and annotating your list. Then spend another block sending messages using your notes.

### B. Big Fast Value

Don't offer a "game planning session" (code for sales call). Offer the most valuable thing you can afford to give away.

> "We swapped from 'game planning' to actually giving them as much free service as we could afford. Our take rates 3x'd."

Make it so good they feel stupid saying no. "Give away stuff people would actually pay for" — not stuff they should pay for.

## Step 3: Volume — More Contacts, More Ways, More Times

### Automate Delivery

Manual: one person handwriting each message.
Automated: send same message to 1,000 people; pre-recorded voicemail drops; email blasts; templated texts.

The higher the personalization, the higher the response rate. But automation unlocks scale. Blend them:
- More personalization for smaller, higher-value lists
- More automation for larger, lower-cost-of-miss lists

### Follow Up Multiple Times, Multiple Ways

Most people contact once and give up. Don't. Think of it like trying to reach your parents in an emergency — you'd call, text, voicemail, repeat.

**Multi-channel follow-up sequence example:**
1. Email
2. LinkedIn DM: "Following up on my email — did you get it?"
3. Phone call
4. Voicemail
5. Final email

**Follow-up logic:**
- They may not have seen your first message
- They may have seen it but the timing was bad
- Their circumstances may have changed (now they have the problem you solve)

**After finishing the full list:** Wait 3–6 months and start again. The same list produces new engaged leads when circumstances change.

### Benchmarks

| Method | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| Cold calls: pick-up rate | ~20% |
| Of those who pick up: take lead magnet | ~25% |
| Engaged leads per 100 calls | ~5 |
| Engaged leads per 100 emails (3% to reply) | ~3 |
| Engaged leads per 100 DMs (personalized) | ~20% |
| Target: CAC vs. LTGP | 1:3 minimum |

**Good result from cold outreach:** 30:1 LTGP:CAC ratio is achievable for well-run operations.

## Platform-Specific Scripts

**Cold email structure:**
1. Subject line with personalization or pattern interrupt
2. 1–2 sentences of personalization (specific to them)
3. One-sentence value hook ("We helped [similar business] get [result] in [timeframe]")
4. One specific ask — not "hop on a call," something smaller first
5. Keep under half a page

**Cold DM / message structure:**
1. Personalized opener (1–2 sentences, shows you know them)
2. Brief mention of who you've helped and how
3. One specific question or low-commitment ask

**Cold call structure:**
1. [Name]? (pause — like a friend)
2. Personalized opener (1–2 sentences)
3. Bridge to why you're calling + social proof
4. Soft benefits question: "You wouldn't be upset if X happened, would you?"
5. Simple ask for a short meeting or next step

## 7 Reasons Cold Outreach Is Underrated

1. No content creation required — one great message, iterated
2. Invisible to competitors — all private, no one sees your playbook
3. Reliable — a fixed number of inputs produces a fixed number of outputs
4. Fewer platform rule changes — cold calling and email rules haven't changed much in 30 years
5. Less compliance headache — no platform banning industries or policing claims
6. Makes business sellable — a cold outreach machine doesn't depend on the founder's face
7. Hard to copy — lists, scripts, software, and team processes are hard to replicate fast

---

## Timeline Expectations

Cold outreach takes longer than most expect. A realistic timeline:
- Months 1–2: Zero to minimal results (still learning)
- Month 3: First consistent results
- Month 6–12: Reliable, scalable output

> "I expected 12 weeks. It took almost a year." — Hormozi

Expect 3 months minimum before judging whether cold outreach is working for your business.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Reaching out once and giving up | Multi-channel, multi-touch — people need multiple exposures |
| Offering a "strategy call" as the lead magnet | Offer something with real standalone value |
| Not personalizing | One specific line about them converts dramatically better |
| Tracking metrics loosely | Track pick-up rate, reply rate, show rate, close rate per platform |
| Hiring people who don't track metrics | Cold outreach failed twice before; the third succeeded because the rep tracked every stat |
