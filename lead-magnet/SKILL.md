# Lead Magnet — Convert Strangers Into Engaged Leads

## Overview

A lead magnet is a complete solution to a narrow problem — typically free or low-cost — that reveals a larger problem your core offer solves. It gets more engaged leads than advertising a core offer directly, and for less money.

**Key principle:** Give away the secrets. Sell the implementation.

---

## Why Lead Magnets Work

Advertising a core offer directly goes straight for the sale. Sometimes that works. For expensive or high-trust offers, a lead magnet works better because:

- A person who pays with **time now** is more likely to pay with **money later**
- Free leads cost less per engaged lead than direct sales ads (often 3–10x cheaper)
- If your lead magnet is excellent, it earns trust before a sale

**The math:** If it costs $1000 in ads to get someone on a sales call and you close 1 in 3, your CAC is $3000. If a lead magnet cuts cost per call to $100 and you close 1 in 10, CAC is $1000 — 3x better for the same ad budget.

---

## 3 Types of Lead Magnets

| Type | When to use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Reveal a problem** | When they don't know they have it | Website speed audit, posture assessment, financial health check |
| **Sample / trial** | When core offer is recurring; best to experience it | Free massage, free first session, free 30-day trial |
| **One step of a multi-step process** | When core offer has multiple stages | First chapter of a book, step 1 of a 3-part system, free module |

---

## 4 Delivery Formats

| Format | Example |
|---|---|
| **Software / tool** | Calculator, dashboard, spreadsheet, app |
| **Information** | Course, guide, video, case study, checklist |
| **Service** | Free audit, free consult, free first session |
| **Physical product** | Book, sample, kit |

**Tip:** Make the same lead magnet in multiple formats. The same content in video + audio + text gets 2–4x the engaged leads for the same effort (different people prefer different formats).

## 7-Step Build Process

### Step 1: Pick the narrow problem and avatar

Identify one specific problem your ideal customer has, one step before your core offer becomes relevant.

- **Example:** Core offer = "we help gym owners fill their gym." Lead magnet problem = "gym owners don't know how to run ads."
- **Test:** After solving the narrow problem, the next obvious problem should be solved by your core offer.

### Step 2: Pick how to solve it

Choose one of the 3 types above. Prefer the type that makes the next problem most obvious.

### Step 3: Pick the delivery format

Start with the format easiest to consume for your audience. Then add other formats over time.

### Step 4: Name it

Name testing is the highest-leverage thing you can do before launch. A name change can 2–10x engagement.

**Test in this order:**
1. **Headline** (the main hook) — test 2 versions
2. **Image** — real vs. illustrated vs. no image
3. **Subheadline** — small word changes matter ("How to" beats just the promise)

**Naming heuristics:**
- Specificity beats cleverness: "How we added 213 members in 30 days" > "How to grow your gym"
- Numbers create curiosity: "7 ways to..." > "Ways to..."
- "How I" beats "How to" — experience can't be questioned

**Test method:** Post two options publicly, ask your audience to pick 1 or 2. Even a small sample gives directional signal. If people ask "when can I have it?" — that's a mega winner.

### Step 5: Make it easy to consume

More formats = more consumption = more conversions.
- Software: Mobile-accessible, multi-device
- Information: Offer video + audio + text
- Services: More time slots, more ways to book
- Physical: Fast shipping, simple packaging, clear instructions

### Step 6: Make it genuinely good

> Give away so much value people feel obligated to pay you.

Mediocre lead magnets destroy trust. 99% of people won't buy — but they'll form an opinion of your brand and tell others. Make your lead magnet as good as your paid product.

### Step 7: CTA — Tell them what to do next

After consuming the lead magnet, some people are ready to buy. Capture them.

Good CTAs have two elements:
1. **Clear action:** "Click here," "Book a call," "Reply YES," "Go to X"
2. **Reason to act now:** Scarcity, urgency, or any reason at all

**Scarcity:** "I can only take 5 new clients this month."

**Urgency:** "This offer closes Friday at midnight."

**Any reason (even weak ones beat no reason):** "Because it's the new year and I want to help people get started."

**9-word email** (Dean Jackson) — for reactivating a warm list:
> "Are you still looking to [4-word desire]?"
> Example: "Are you still looking to grow your gym?"

## Lead Magnet Quality Test

Would your best customer pay for this?
- Yes → good enough to give away free
- No → it's not valuable enough yet; improve it

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Making the lead magnet vague or generic | Solve ONE narrow problem for ONE specific avatar |
| Giving it away but making the CTA weak | Every piece needs a clear next step with a reason to act |
| Only one format | Offer video + audio + text — 2–4x the leads for the same work |
| Good lead magnet, weak name | Test the name before investing in the content |
| Giving away fluff to "protect secrets" | Fluff destroys trust; give real value and you earn the sale |
