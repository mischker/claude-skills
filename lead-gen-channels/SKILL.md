# Lead Generation Channels — $100M Leads Framework

## Overview

The right channel depends on three things: who your customer is, where they already spend attention, and what your offer requires to convert. Most businesses fail at lead gen by using the wrong channel, not by having a bad offer.

**Core principle:** Find where your customer already congregates, meet them there with something they want.

---

## The Four Lead Gen Categories

Hormozi breaks lead gen into four categories based on who does the outreach:

| Category | Who does the work | Speed | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Warm outreach** | You (to people you know) | Fast | Free |
| **Cold outreach** | You (to strangers) | Medium | Low |
| **Content / organic** | Your content attracts | Slow | Low (but time) |
| **Paid advertising** | Platform distributes | Fast | High |

**Start here:** Warm outreach → Cold outreach → Content → Paid ads (in that order, for most bootstrapped businesses).

---

## Warm Outreach (Start Here)

**Best for:** Launching a new offer; validating before spending money

**If you have no warm list:** Build one first (2–4 weeks). Reconnect with past colleagues, classmates, clients via LinkedIn. Ask current contacts "who do you know who has [problem]?" Attend 2–3 industry events. Join 2–3 relevant communities and contribute for a few weeks before reaching out. Only move to cold outreach after you've exhausted warm.

Who: People who already know you — past customers, professional contacts, friends, followers

How:
1. List everyone who fits your avatar (or who knows someone who does)
2. Reach out personally with a genuine message
3. Ask if they (or anyone they know) might benefit

Script:
> "Hey [name] — I'm working with [type of person] to help them [outcome]. Do you know anyone dealing with [problem]? I'd love an introduction. Happy to return the favor."

Never mass-blast. Personal, specific, one-at-a-time.

## Cold Outreach

**Best for:** B2B, high-ticket, specific niche with identifiable prospects

Channels: LinkedIn, email, Instagram DMs, cold calls

**The 3-step cold message:**
1. Compliment or specific observation (shows you're real)
2. Value hook (what's in it for them)
3. One specific ask (not "hop on a call" — something smaller)

> "Hi [Name] — I saw your post about [specific thing]. Most [their role] I talk to struggle with [problem]. I helped [similar company] solve it in [timeframe]. Would a quick breakdown of how we did it be useful to you?"

**Volume is the game:** Cold outreach is a numbers game. 100 messages → 5–10 responses → 1–3 calls → 1 customer. Track and improve each step.

## Organic / Content

**Best for:** Long-term lead flow; thought leadership; lower-ticket offers

**Platform match:**

| Platform | Best for | Content type |
|---|---|---|
| YouTube | Education-heavy products; long sales cycles | Long-form video |
| Instagram / TikTok | B2C, visual products, lifestyle brands | Short video |
| LinkedIn | B2B, professional services, consulting | Written posts, video |
| Twitter/X | Thought leaders, tech, creators | Short text, threads |
| Podcast | Authority building; long-form audiences | Audio |
| Email newsletter | High-trust, high-conversion | Written |
| SEO / Blog | High-intent search; evergreen | Long-form written |

**The content formula (Hormozi):**
- Document what you're doing (easier than creating from scratch)
- Teach one specific thing per piece
- End every piece with a clear next step (CTA)
- Post consistently for at least 6 months before judging channel

## Paid Advertising

**Best for:** Scaling a proven offer; businesses with positive 30-day unit economics

**Only run paid ads when:** You have a working offer with a positive CAC-to-30-day-revenue ratio. Paid ads amplify what already works — they can't save a broken offer.

**Platform match:**

| Platform | Best for | Audience targeting |
|---|---|---|
| Meta (FB/IG) | B2C, broad consumer audiences, local | Interest, demographic, lookalike |
| Google Search | High-intent buyers searching for solutions | Keyword-based |
| YouTube ads | Education, courses, B2B at scale | Interest + demographic |
| LinkedIn ads | B2B, high-ticket professional services | Job title, company size, industry |
| TikTok ads | Young B2C, viral consumer products | Interest + behavior |

**Ad formula:**
1. Hook (stops the scroll)
2. Problem (they recognize themselves)
3. Mechanism (how you solve it differently)
4. Offer (clear CTA + what they get)
5. Urgency or proof

## Lead Magnets

**A lead magnet converts cold traffic into warm leads.**

Best lead magnets for each business type:

| Business type | Lead magnet |
|---|---|
| Consulting / coaching | Free audit, assessment, or strategy call |
| Course / education | Free mini-course, guide, or template |
| SaaS | Free trial, free plan, or demo |
| E-commerce | Discount code, free sample, quiz result |
| Local service | Free consultation, free first session |
| Content creator | Free guide, checklist, or email course |

**Lead magnet quality test:** Would your best customer pay for this? If yes, it's good enough to give away free.

## Referrals

**Highest-converting, lowest-cost lead source. Most businesses ignore it.**

How to systematize:

1. Ask every happy customer: "Who else do you know who'd benefit from [outcome]?"
2. Make it easy: "Can I send you a quick message you could forward to them?"
3. Incentivize: referral discount, credit, gift, or public recognition
4. Make it a process: ask at the first win, at 30 days, at 90 days

**Rollover Gift Card play:** Sell gift cards at 90% off. Customer gives them to friends. See `mm-rollover-upsell`.

## Channel Selection Framework

Answer these questions to pick your channel:

1. **Is your offer B2B or B2C?**
   - B2B → LinkedIn, cold email, referrals, events
   - B2C → Meta, TikTok, YouTube, Google, referrals

2. **Is your buyer actively searching for a solution?**
   - Yes → Google Search ads, SEO
   - No → Interrupt-based (Meta, TikTok, YouTube)

3. **Is your ticket price high (> $1,000) or low?**
   - High → Cold outreach, referrals, content → sales call
   - Low → Paid ads, content → self-serve purchase

4. **Do you have money to spend or time to spend?**
   - Money → Paid ads
   - Time → Organic content, cold outreach, warm outreach

5. **How fast do you need results?**
   - Fast → Warm outreach, cold outreach, paid ads
   - OK with slow build → Content, SEO, referral systems

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Starting with paid ads before validating offer | Validate with warm/cold outreach first |
| Being on every platform | Pick one channel, master it, then expand |
| Content without CTA | Every piece needs a clear next step |
| No referral system | Build referral ask into your standard process |
| Measuring leads, not customers | Track leads → booked calls → closed customers by channel |
