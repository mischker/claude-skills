# Continuity Bonus Offer — Continuity Offer

## Overview

Give customers something valuable (bonus) when they sign up for a recurring payment today. The bonus's value should exceed the first month's payment. This bridges the gap between one-time purchases and ongoing recurring revenue.

**Core mechanic:** Advertise the bonus, not the membership. Once they want the bonus, reveal it's free with membership.

**Timing:** Use LAST in your money model sequence — after Attraction, Upsell, and Downsell offers have been made.

**Which continuity offer to use:**

| Offer | Use when |
|---|---|
| **Continuity Bonus** (this skill) | You have something valuable to give away; customer motivated by getting more |
| **Continuity Discount** (`mm-continuity-discount`) | You want to give free time or lower rate in exchange for a commitment |
| **Waived Fee** (`mm-waived-fee`) | High-ticket B2B or long-term service; commitment enforced by fee avoidance |

## Structure

**Step 1: Design the bonus**
- Must relate to your core offer (wrong bonuses attract wrong customers)
- Must be independently valuable — something you've sold or could sell separately
- Can be a one-time bonus (onboarding, past content library) or ongoing monthly perk
- Use things you already have/do — zero extra cost to deliver

**Step 2: Price everything**
Every bonus needs a dollar value. No exceptions. Unpriced bonuses anchor nothing.

**Step 3: Sell the bonus, then reveal the membership**
> "Before I let you go, I want to offer you something. We have [bonus worth $X] — [explain value]. Would you like to know how you can get that for free?"

They say yes. Then:
> "When you become a [member/subscriber] today, you get all of that as a free gift for joining. Or, you can just buy [bonus] for $[X] — which would you prefer?"

## Stacking Bonuses

More bonuses = more people join.

> "On top of that, as a member you also get: [amazing thing 1 — $X value], [amazing thing 2 — $Y value], [amazing thing 3 — $Z value]. Total member value: $[sum]. For just $[monthly rate]/month."

Mention individual prices. Stacking anchors compound.

## Pricing for Continuity vs. One-Time

If you offer both standalone and continuity:

| Standalone markup vs. continuity | % who choose continuity |
|---|---|
| 1.33x | 50% |
| 1.66x | 60% |
| 2x | 70% |
| 2.33x | 80% |
| 2.66x | 90% |

The bigger the standalone vs. continuity price difference, the more people choose the subscription.

## Bulk Prepaid Upsell (to Boost 30-Day Profit)

After they agree to continuity:
> "By the way — if you prepay for 5 months, you get the 6th free. Want to do that?"

Only 1 in 8 people needs to take this to raise 30-day profits by 50%.

## Title System Alternative

If you can't create tangible bonuses, give status titles: Silver, Gold, Diamond at 3/6/12 month milestones. Customers will introduce themselves by title. Free to implement; high perceived value.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Advertising the membership | Advertise the bonus; reveal membership after interest |
| Bonus unrelated to core offer | Wrong bonus attracts wrong customers who churn fast |
| No dollar value on bonuses | Price every bonus; unpriced = worthless in customer's mind |
| Offering continuity as attraction offer | Use continuity last; without up-front cash from earlier offers, advertising is unprofitable |
| No commitment option | Offer both month-to-month and prepaid; let pricing guide choice |
