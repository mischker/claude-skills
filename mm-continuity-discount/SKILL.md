# Continuity Discount Offer — Continuity Offer

## Overview

Give customers free service time (or a discount) in exchange for committing to a longer subscription. The discount can be applied upfront, spread across payments, given at the end, or triggered after the first 1–2 payments.

**Core mechanic:** "Buy X months, get Y free." The more free time, the more people commit. The timing of when they get the free time affects churn and cash flow.

## The Four Ways to Apply the Discount

| Method | Cash impact | Churn impact | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Upfront** | Delayed (free period first) | Higher (easy to get and leave) | Industries with strong contract enforcement |
| **At the end** | Immediate (they pay first) | Lower (must earn free time) | Services where outcome takes time |
| **Spread over term** | Steady cash flow | Moderate | Most subscription businesses |
| **After 1–2 payments** | Small upfront, then normal | Low | Validates payment; covers advertising cost |

## The "Highest Value Note in the Book"

**Bill weekly (every 4 weeks), not monthly.**
12 months = 12 payments. 4-week cycles = 13 payments. Same price perception, 8.3% more revenue.

If margins are 20%, this adds **41% to annual profit** with zero additional work. Just change the words.

## Lifetime Discount at Churn Point

1. Find average month when customers cancel (e.g., month 4)
2. Offer a lifetime discount that activates at month 5
3. Tell all customers upfront: "Stay to month 5 and your rate drops forever"
4. As month 5 approaches: send a reminder — "Your lower rate is almost here"

This converts your highest-churn moment into a retention trigger.

## Cancellation Policy Options

Best policy: **Cancellation fee = the total discount they received**

> "You got $600 in discounts by committing. If you cancel early, you pay $600 to cover those. If you complete the term, the fee goes away."

Other options: 30/60-day notice, cancel anytime, fixed cancellation fee.

Make cancellation visible and easy — silent cancellers leave bad reviews. Easy cancellation = more at-bats to save them.

## Exit Interview to Save Cancellations

> "I'll waive your cancellation fee if you spend 10 minutes telling me what went wrong."

Saves ~1/3 of customers who agree to the interview. At minimum, you learn what to fix.

## Gift Card Variation

Give discount as a physical gift card instead of a price reduction:
- Customers can use it themselves later OR give to a friend
- Unused gift cards = full-priced sign-up (pure upside)
- Friend gift = referral + new lead

## Processing Fee Revenue

> "It's $[X] plus a 3% processing fee."

Nobody ever refuses. If margins are 10%, adding 3% = 30% more profit. Trivial words, material impact.

## Two Payment Methods

Get a second form of payment (ideally ACH) to prevent churn from failed payments.

> "Do you want to save the 3% processing fee? Give us a second form of payment. We only charge the fee when we have to manually chase down payment info — so if you save us the hassle, we pass the savings to you."

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Frontloading discount on high-churn product | Use end-loaded or after-payment discount instead |
| Monthly billing (not 4-week) | Switch to 4-week cycles — free 8.3% revenue |
| No clear cancellation path | Make it visible; otherwise they complain publicly |
| Single payment method | Always get two; ACH preferred for second |
| No lifetime discount trigger | Place it just past average churn month |
