# Customer Retention — Churn Reduction System

## Overview

Reducing churn from 9% to 3% is a 3.3x increase in lifetime value — without spending a dollar on acquisition. It costs 5–25x more to acquire a new customer than retain one.

**The core equation:**
- Referrals > churn → business grows without paid advertising
- Referrals = churn → you need ads to grow
- Referrals < churn → you need ads just to stay flat

**The diagnostic question:** Instead of "How do I retain customers?" ask "What would make every customer cancel?" Then do the exact opposite.

---

## The 5 Horsemen of Retention

Derived from interviewing gym owners with <3% monthly churn. Five common factors appeared across all of them:

1. **Track attendance (engagement):** Customers who use the product 3x/week stay. Those at 1–2x/week churn. Monitor engagement frequency. When it drops from 3x to 2x — intervene immediately. That's the rescue window. Miss it and they cancel.

2. **Reach out 2x per week:** Praise customers for participation and progress. Solve small problems. Do both. The cadence matters more than the message.

3. **Handwritten cards:** Send at signup, at referral asks (3/6/12 month milestones), birthdays, and holidays. Never a bad time to send one.

4. **Member events:** Run internal events on a regular but unpredictable schedule. Every 21, 42, or 63 days. Use handwritten invites. Events reduce churn AND generate referrals simultaneously.

5. **Exit interviews:** Require exit interviews before cancellation (set this expectation at onboarding). Conduct before they leave. Saves ~50% of phone/email cancellations. Even if only half show: ~25% churn reduction = 33% LTV increase.

**Warning:** When you implement the 5 Horsemen, churn goes UP in month 1 (billing reactivation + people who already churned mentally). Then drops 50% in month 2, 50% again in month 3.

## Churn Checklist (9 Steps)

### 1. Find Your Activation Point
An activation point is a specific action or result that predicts whether a customer stays.

Template: *Every customer who does [X thing] or gets [Y result] stays 3x longer than those who don't.*

**How to find it:**
- Find customers who stayed 3+ months and paid the most
- Look at their demographic/usage data
- Find 2–3 common behaviors that set them apart
- Build your onboarding to drive every new customer to those behaviors

Examples:
- B2B service: first time they get a lead from your service
- SaaS: first time they log in and see results on the dashboard
- Physical product: first time they use and feel the result
- Community: first time they make a post and get engagement

### 2. Onboard Customers
Onboarding = teaching customers how to hit the activation point.

**Hierarchy:** Custom > generic. Personal > group. Live > recorded. Carrots > sticks. Something > nothing.

**Minimum onboarding:**
- Tell them exactly how to get value → drive to activation point
- Resell the value of the purchase, framed in their goals
- Tell them how they unlock more value as they stay longer
- Tell them best practices to communicate with you and other customers
- Establish when you'll next speak to them

### 3. Incentivize Activation
Make the first activation feel like a win. Stack it with recognition, status, or access that only comes after hitting the activation point.

### 4. Community / Events
Connect customers to each other. Community creates social accountability and belonging — both of which reduce churn. Member events reinforce both.

### 5. Fire or Correct Bad Customers
Bad customers increase churn for everyone else. Your worst customers: complain most, pay least, get worst results. Either coach them to fit the program or exit them gracefully. Better-fit customers stay longer and send referrals.

### 6. Annual Pricing Options
Offer annual prepay at a discount. Customers who pay annually churn at dramatically lower rates (sunk cost + perceived commitment). Creates predictable cash + lower monthly churn.

### 7. Cancellation Call / Video
When someone requests cancellation, require a brief call before processing. Even a recorded exit interview video works. Many cancel impulsively; a conversation resurfaces the original reason they joined.

### 8. Survey Customers Regularly
Regular NPS or satisfaction surveys surface problems before they become cancellations. Find your most common complaints → fix the top one monthly.

Process:
1. Gather customer service data + surveys + reviews → find most common problem
2. Interview customers who worked around it → get their solution
3. Fix → test → roll out → repeat with next problem

### 9. Four-Step Customer Journey
Map: Activation → Early win → Ongoing value → Ascension. Every customer should know where they are and what comes next.

## The Less-Is-More Principle

"Think value per second, not seconds of value."

Overwhelm is the #1 reason for churn. Adding more content, more calls, more features almost always increases churn. Less but better is better than more and mediocre.

When Gym Launch added more calls, churn went up. When they cut to one Q&A/month + one physical newsletter, churn went down.

Decide on 2–3 core things you deliver. Make them excellent. Delete the rest.

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly churn | Lost customers ÷ starting customers | <3% for B2B, <5% for B2C |
| Retention rate | 1 - churn | >95% B2B, >95% B2C |
| LTV impact of churn reduction | Gross profit per month ÷ churn | Improve 1 point = disproportionate LTV gain |

Cutting churn from 10% to 3%: customer stays 10 months → 33 months. Same product, same price, 3.3x the revenue per customer.
