# Payment Plan Downsell — Downsell Offer

## Overview

When a customer says no due to price, offer the same product at the same total price — but spread across scheduled payments. Don't discount. Split the cost.

**Core mechanic:** "Too expensive" usually means "too much upfront." Payment plans let customers pay the same total while paying less now.

**Key rule:** Never lower the price. Change when and how they pay, not what they pay total.

## The 7-Step Downsell Process

Start at full-price, work toward more distributed payment over time. Stop when they buy.

**Step 1 — Reward prepayment (not punish payment plans)**
Present price with interest baked in, then offer a discount for paying upfront:
> "It's $[price with interest]. But if you pay today, you save $[X] — that's what most people do."

**Step 2 — 3rd-Party Financing / Credit Card / Layaway**
- 3rd party: company pays you now; customer pays them over time
- Credit card: "Would you rather you decide your payment terms, or should I?" → they say credit card; you get paid today
- Layaway: customer pays off item before receiving it; zero risk to you

**Step 3 — Half now, half on their next paycheck**
> "When do you get paid? OK — put half down today and the rest when you get paid. Fair?"

Ask for the largest possible down payment. Schedule on paycheck dates (not monthly billing).

**Step 4 — Check if they still want it (1–10 scale)**
> "On a scale from 1–10, how much do you want this?"

- 8–10: keep downselling → "We'll figure out a way to make this work."
- 7 or below: pivot to Feature Downsell → "Let's find something that's a better fit for where you are."

**Step 5 — Three payments (⅓ now, ⅓ paycheck 2, ⅓ paycheck 3)**

**Step 6 — Equal payments spread over the service duration**
(e.g., 16-week program = 16 weekly payments)

**Step 7 — Free Trial With Penalty**
Card on file, start for free, pay if criteria missed. See `mm-trial-with-penalty`.

## Billing Cadence Effect on Churn (Profitwell data, 14,000 businesses)

| Billing frequency | Monthly churn rate |
|---|---|
| Monthly | 10.7% |
| Quarterly | 5% |
| Annual | 2% |

→ Always start with annual / largest upfront. Work down only if they say no.

## "Seesaw" Simplified Version

> "Would you rather have giant monthly payments or tiny ones?" → they say tiny.
> "We can start with $[down] now and adjust the rest so monthly feels manageable. How much can you put down today?"

Pull chair to their side of the table. Make it collaborative.

## Important Notes

- Align payment dates with paycheck dates — fewer declined payments
- Run declined cards multiple times the same day (different deposit windows)
- Offer mid-plan upsell: "You can still get the prepay discount if you pay your balance now"
- Don't let people who would've paid in full switch to payment plans → close rate should rise, paid-in-full % should stay flat

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Discounting instead of splitting | Never lower total price; only split timing |
| Skipping Step 4 (desire check) | Downselling someone who doesn't want it wastes time |
| Monthly billing → high churn | Always start with annual; monthly is last resort |
| Not rewarding prepay | Frame payment plan as the "interest" version; prepay = discount |
