# Lifetime Value — The Crazy Eight

## Overview

**Why LTV is the ultimate business lever:** The business owner who can make a customer more valuable to his business than to his competition wins. He can outspend everyone on acquisition and get all the customers.

"Costs approach zero, but revenue can go to infinity."

**Best upsell formula:** More of — or more help with — what they just bought, but with faster results, less risk, less effort, less hassle — for more money.

---

## How to Calculate LTGP

**Step 1: Gross Profit per Transaction**

```
Gross Profit = Revenue - Direct Delivery Cost

Example: $3,000/month service, $600/month to deliver = $2,400 gross profit per customer per month
```

**Step 2: Number of Transactions or Churn**

For transactional businesses: export customer data, sort by # purchases, average the column.

For recurring businesses: calculate monthly churn.
```
Churn = customers lost ÷ original customer count
Example: 100 customers, lost 5 = 5% churn
```

**Step 3: Calculate LTGP**

```
Transactional: Gross profit per transaction × avg transactions = LTGP
Example: $80 × 4 purchases = $320 LTGP

Recurring: Gross profit per period ÷ churn rate = LTGP
Example: $2,400 ÷ 5% = $48,000 LTGP
```

## The Crazy Eight — 8 Ways to Increase LTV

### 1. Raise Prices
Pricing affects gross profit more than any other lever. A 20% price increase on a 10% margin business doesn't grow revenue by 20% — it **triples** profit. Test prices 20% higher every 10 sales until conversion drops materially.

### 2. Lower Cost to Deliver
Reduce how much it costs to fulfill what you already sell. Systematize delivery, batch processes, automate, renegotiate suppliers. Same revenue, more gross profit.

### 3. Upsell Frequency — Get them to buy more again later
Sell your next offer to existing customers after they've gotten value. The best next offer is: more of what they just bought, but more intensive/personalized/faster.

How: Build a "what's next" offer at every milestone. Create a natural ascension path.

### 4. Cross-Sell — Get them to buy a different thing
Sell something adjacent to what they already bought. Key rule: **sell the next problem your customers have, not the next product you want to build.**

Process: Survey current customers. Ask "What would you most want help with next?" Take the top answer. Build that. Don't guess.

### 5. Upsell Quantity — Get them to buy more now
Sell a larger quantity at a per-unit discount. Works for physical products, bulk service hours, prepaid sessions, annual plans.

### 6. Upsell Quality — Get them to buy premium
Offer a higher-tier version with better service, faster results, more attention, more access, more customization — at a higher price.

### 7. Downsell Quantity — Get them to buy fewer rather than nothing
When a customer says no to the full offer, offer less. Fewer sessions. Shorter duration. Lower quantity. Keep the sale.

### 8. Downsell Quality — Get them to buy lower-tier rather than nothing
Offer a stripped-down version at a lower price. Lite plan, DIY version, starter tier. Capture customers who can't afford the core offer.

## Decision Framework

**Which lever to pull first?**

1. Check LTGP:CAC ratio first
   - If LTGP:CAC < 3:1 → fix LTV levers before running more ads
   - If LTGP:CAC ≥ 3:1 → focus on acquisition volume

2. Check what bottleneck exists:
   - One-time buyers, no repeat → Lever 3 (frequency) or Lever 4 (cross-sell)
   - Low average order value → Lever 1 (price) or Lever 5 (quantity)
   - High churn → see `customer-retention` skill
   - Low gross margins → Lever 2 (lower delivery cost)

3. Test next product with existing customers before building:
   - Survey first: show both your idea and what customers actually want
   - If they don't vote for yours, don't build it

---

## The 2.2x Rule

Adding a single strong backend product that 1 in 5 customers buy at 50% of front-end price = 2.2x LTGP. This allows 2x+ ad spend at the same CAC — effectively doubling or tripling revenue.

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Building next product based on founder passion | Survey customers. They vote with their wallets |
| Only one thing to sell | You cap what any customer can spend |
| Raising price on new customers only | Test new prices on a cohort, then roll out to existing base |
| Treating all customers the same | Your top 20% generates 80% of revenue. Find and serve them more |
| Ignoring churn when calculating LTV | LTGP = gross profit / churn. Cutting churn from 9% to 3% = 3.3x LTV |
