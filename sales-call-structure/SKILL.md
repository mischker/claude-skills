# Sales Call Structure — Hormozi Framework

## Overview

A sales call is a diagnostic conversation that ends in a prescription. You're not selling — you're finding out if you can help, and if you can, telling them what to do. The more it sounds like a doctor's appointment, the better it converts.

**Core frame:** You're the expert. They came to you. Your job is to find the problem and recommend the right solution — not convince them to buy.

---

## Pre-Call Checklist

```
[ ] Know their situation (from application, intake form, prior research)
[ ] Know your full offer stack (attraction + upsells + downsells + continuity)
[ ] Know your prices and payment plan options
[ ] Have calendar open to book start date during the call
[ ] Quiet space, no interruptions
```

---

## Call Structure (60-Minute Format)

### Phase 1: Rapport + Agenda (5 min)

Open with a genuine observation or question. Then set the agenda:

> "Good to meet you. I had a quick look at your [intake form / application] — [specific thing you noticed]. Before we dive in, here's what I want to do: I'm going to ask you some questions to understand where you are and where you want to be. If I think I can help, I'll tell you exactly what that looks like and what it costs. If I don't think it's a fit, I'll tell you that too. Sound good?"

This sets you up as the expert, not the salesperson.

---

### Phase 2: Diagnosis — Where They Are Now (10 min)

Find the gap between current state and desired state.

**Current state questions:**
- "Walk me through where things are right now with [problem area]."
- "What have you tried so far? What happened?"
- "What's this costing you — time, money, stress — right now?"

**Desired state questions:**
- "If we solve this, what does that look like 6 months from now?"
- "What's the specific outcome you need?"
- "Why is this important right now vs. waiting another year?"

**Urgency qualifier:**
- "On a scale of 1–10, how important is solving this right now?"
- If below 7: "What would make it a 10?" → either they reveal real urgency, or this call is premature

---

### Phase 3: Bridge — Identifying the Root Problem (10 min)

Connect what they told you to what's actually causing it.

> "Based on what you've shared — it sounds like the real issue is [root problem], not just [surface symptom]. Does that feel right?"

This positions you as someone who understands them deeply. Builds trust before the pitch.

---

### Phase 4: The Pitch (10 min)

Lead with outcome. Then explain mechanism. Then present offer.

**Outcome first:**
> "Here's what we'd do for you. In [X weeks/months], you'd have [specific outcome they said they wanted]."

**Mechanism:**
> "The way we do that is [brief, specific process — 3 steps max]. We've done this with [type of customer] and they get [specific result]."

**Offer:**
> "For you specifically, I'd recommend [specific package]. Here's what's included: [list]. And the investment is [price]."

Don't hedge. Present with confidence. Then stop talking.

---

### Phase 5: Handle Objections (10–15 min)

See `sales-objection-handler` for full scripts.

**Core principle:** Ask before answering. Identify the real objection, then address only that.

After addressing: always close again — with the same assumptive energy as the original close:
> "Does that address your concern? Great." [Pause one second.] "So — [Option A] or [Option B]? Which card do you want to use?"

Don't ask permission again. Assume the yes is back on. The objection was handled; the decision stands.

---

### Phase 6: Close + Upsell (10 min)

Once they say yes to the attraction offer:

**Close:**
> "Great. I'll grab your card and we'll get you set up. What card do you want to use?"

**Immediately upsell:**
> "Before we finalize — most people at this stage also [next problem]. We have [upsell offer] that handles that. Want to add that now?"

See `mm-classic-upsell` or `mm-anchor-upsell` for upsell scripts.

---

### Phase 7: Downsell if No (5–10 min)

If they say no to the main offer:
1. Payment Plan Downsell first → `mm-payment-plan-downsell`
2. Feature Downsell if still no → `mm-feature-downsell`
3. Trial With Penalty as last resort → `mm-trial-with-penalty`

---

### Phase 8: Continuity + Next Steps (5 min)

After purchase is confirmed:

**Set expectations:**
> "Here's exactly what happens next: [Step 1, Step 2, Step 3]. Your first [call / session / deliverable] will be on [date]."

**Offer continuity if applicable:**
> "One more thing — most people who complete [program] stay with us after because [next problem is solved by continuity]. Here's what that looks like..."

See `mm-continuity-bonus` or `mm-waived-fee`.

**Book the first session before hanging up:**
Never leave a call without the next action confirmed and on calendar.

---

## Shorter Call (30-Minute Format)

For warm leads or simpler offers:

| Phase | Time |
|---|---|
| Rapport + agenda | 2 min |
| Diagnosis (current + desired) | 8 min |
| Pitch | 5 min |
| Objections + close | 10 min |
| Next steps | 5 min |

---

## Call Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Show rate | > 80% |
| Offer rate (made a pitch) | > 90% of calls |
| Close rate | > 30% |
| Upsell rate | > 25% of closes |
| No-show follow-up conversion | > 15% |

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Pitching before diagnosing | Ask questions for at least 10 minutes before presenting |
| Hedging on price ("it depends") | Know your prices; present confidently |
| Accepting first "no" | Always ask what's behind the no; one objection to address |
| Ending call without next action | Book first session before hanging up |
| Forgetting upsell after close | The upsell always comes immediately after yes |
