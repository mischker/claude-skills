# Waived Fee Offer — Continuity Offer

## Overview

Present two options: (A) pay a large setup fee and go month-to-month, or (B) commit to a longer term and get the setup fee waived. If they cancel early, they pay the waived fee. Customers stay because leaving costs more than staying.

**Core mechanic:** Loss aversion. People don't want to pay a fee they already avoided. The fee makes leaving psychologically and financially expensive.

## Structure

**Option A — Month-to-Month with Fee**
> "$[monthly rate]/month + $[setup fee] today. Cancel whenever you want."

**Option B — Commitment with Fee Waived**
> "$[monthly rate]/month, no setup fee, committed to [12 months]. If you cancel early, the $[setup fee] applies."

Most customers choose B — they get to avoid the fee and maintain the same monthly rate.

## Fee Sizing

| Fee relative to monthly rate | Effect |
|---|---|
| 1.5–3x | More month-to-month sign-ups; more upfront cash |
| 3–5x (recommended) | Most customers commit to avoid the fee |
| 5x+ | Very few month-to-month; maximum commitment pull |

**Default: Set fee at 3–5x monthly rate**

Example: $1,000/month → $3,000–$5,000 setup fee

## Commitment Length

**Minimum: 12 months.** Longer commitments work better.

Works especially well for services where results take time: SEO, investing, weight loss, B2B software, coaching.

The longer the service takes to show results, the more this offer protects both parties — customer can't bail before results appear.

## What Happens at End of Commitment

If they complete the full term → fee goes away permanently. They've earned flexible cancellation.

> "Once you've made all 12 payments, the setup fee disappears. You can cancel anytime after that, no charge."

## Early Cancellation Script

> "You're welcome to cancel. Because you chose the waived-fee option, the $[fee] would apply — same as if you had started month-to-month. Totally your call. But your lower rate is X months away if you want to stick it out."

## Advanced: Cause-Based Cancellation Fee

For maximum motivation:
> "What cause do you absolutely hate? If you cancel early, I'll donate your setup fee to them."

Two reasons to stay: don't lose the money, don't fund a cause they hate.

## Re-engagement After Cancellation

If a customer completes their commitment, cancels, then wants to rejoin later:
- They restart with the month-to-month option (fee applies), OR
- Offer a new commitment to waive the fee again

This prevents gaming (cancel after year 1, rejoin for free fee waive indefinitely).

## If > 5% Cancel Early

Product problem, not pricing problem. The fee nudges people but can't override genuine dissatisfaction. Fix the product.

## When to Use This

| Use when... | Don't use when... |
|---|---|
| Service takes months to deliver results | Quick-outcome products (7-day challenge) |
| B2B / high-ticket ($500+/mo) | Low-price subscriptions where fee feels disproportionate |
| Long-term relationship-based service | One-time deliverable (fee commitment makes no sense) |
| You want committed customers only | You want maximum sign-up volume |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Fee too small → no commitment pull | Minimum 3x monthly; 5x for maximum effect |
| Commitment < 12 months | Longer = better; results take time, fee keeps them committed |
| Not explaining fee logic | "It costs us to onboard you — commit and we cover that cost" |
| Fee stays forever after commitment | Drop it after they complete the term — it's earned |
