# Ads Scaling — More Leads From What Already Works

## Overview

If you're not getting enough engaged leads, you're not doing the core four with enough **skill** or **volume**. The fix is always one of three things:

1. **More** — do more of what already works
2. **Better** — improve the weakest step
3. **New** — expand to new placements, platforms, or methods

Use them in that order. Exhaust "more" and "better" before adding "new."

---

## The Size of the Pie Fallacy

Most entrepreneurs plateau and assume they've saturated their market. Example:
- $2M/year business
- $30K/month on Facebook only
- No content, no cold outreach, no other platforms

They say: "I've saturated the chiropractic niche."

Reality: A competitor in the same niche was spending $30K in advertising *per week* across 4 platforms.

The tiny slice of attention you advertise to is not the entire market. There are more leads than you can possibly imagine — you're just not reaching them.

---

## More

**Double inputs → roughly double outputs** (without changing anything else).

The Rule of 100: Do 100 primary actions per day, for 100 days straight.

| Method | 100 actions |
|---|---|
| Warm outreach | 100 reach-outs per day |
| Content | 100 minutes per day creating content; at least 1 post/day |
| Cold outreach | 100 reach-outs per day |
| Paid ads | 100 minutes/day on ads; keep running for 100 days |

"You will never go hungry again" if you follow the Rule of 100 for 100 days straight.

**Open to Goal** — advanced version:
Instead of committing to X actions, commit to X *outcomes* — no matter how long it takes.
- "I sign 5 new clients today" (not "I make 50 cold calls")
- "I post until I have 3 pieces of content" (not "I work for 2 hours")

This unlocks a new level of effort. You'll do 5,000 flyers instead of 300 because that's what it takes. "Do what is required, not what's comfortable."

## Better

Getting better gets you more leads for the same effort. The only way to get better is to **test**.

### Find the Constraint

Every step in your funnel has a drop-off rate. The step with the biggest drop-off is the **constraint** — the bottleneck where the smallest improvement creates the biggest gain.

**Example:**
| Step | Rate | Improvement |
|---|---|---|
| Optin | 30% | → 35% = 16% more leads |
| **Apply** | **5% ← constraint** | **→ 10% = 100% more leads** |
| Schedule | 50% | → 55% = 10% more leads |

Always test the constraint first. Don't waste tests on non-bottleneck steps.

### Testing Protocol

**One test per week per platform.** Four reasons:
1. Multiple simultaneous changes = impossible to know what worked
2. Changes at one step affect other steps (undetectable with multiple changes)
3. Forces you to prioritize your highest-leverage test
4. One week is typically enough data

**Weekly testing cycle:**
1. **Monday:** Launch one split test per platform
2. **Following Monday:** Review results; pick winner; log in test log (never start from scratch again)
3. Launch next test against the new winner

**When to move on:** If you can't beat your current best version after 4 tests (one month) at one step, move to the next constraint.

**Test log:** Track every test result. You start each new test "a zillion improvements later" rather than at square one.

## New

Only after you've exhausted "more" and "better" — when marginal returns from improving the current method drop below what a new placement/platform/method would return.

### Order of Expansion

1. **New placements** — same platform, different ad formats
   - Instagram: Stories, Reels, DMs, Feed, Messenger
   - YouTube: Shorts, Long-form, Community posts
2. **New platforms** — same content/ads, different distribution
   - YouTube Shorts → Instagram Reels (minimal extra effort)
3. **New core four activity** — add a completely new method (e.g., add cold outreach to a business that only posts content)

**Decision rule:** "What will get me the most leads for the amount of work?"

---

## How the Core Four Compounds

Each method you add boosts the others:
- Free content nurtures paid ad leads (78% of customers consumed 2+ content pieces before buying)
- Paid ads reach people who then find your content and trust you more
- Warm outreach works better if people have seen your content before you reach out
- Cold outreach response rates go up when the prospect has seen your content

A business running paid ads + content will always outperform one doing either alone.

---

## When to Stop Optimizing Ads

Costs can only approach zero. Revenue can go to infinity. Once your CAC is within 3x industry average, you're better off increasing LTGP (better offer, upsells, retention) than trying to cut acquisition costs further.

"You can't save your way to a billion dollars."

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Testing multiple things at once | One test per week per platform — otherwise you learn nothing |
| Switching methods before doing enough volume | "It doesn't work" = usually just not enough reps. 300 flyers ≠ a flyer campaign. |
| Adding "new" before exhausting "more + better" | New = last resort, not first instinct |
| Not keeping a test log | Log every test; start each from your current best, not zero |
| Assuming your niche is saturated | Almost never true at the $1M–$10M level |
