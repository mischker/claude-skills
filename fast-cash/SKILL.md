# Fast Cash — Premium Offer to Warm Audience

## Overview

Fast Cash plays are limited-time, high-ticket offers promoted exclusively to existing customers, past customers, and engaged leads. You've already paid to acquire these people — selling to them again costs almost nothing.

**The 10% × 10x Rule:** Get 10% of your customers to pay 10x your normal price → double your revenue. All extra revenue (minus delivery) drops straight to profit.

**Why it works:**
1. No acquisition cost — list already acquired
2. High-ticket prices have better margins
3. Scarcity + urgency creates fast decisions
4. Existing customers trust you and have already demonstrated willingness to spend

---

## What to Sell

Think: **unscalable access to you or your best people.** The point is to charge for things you wouldn't normally do at scale.

Elements to package:
- **Attention:** 1-on-1 time with you or senior staff; many-on-1 intensive
- **Personalization:** Customized version of your standard solution
- **Convenience:** 24/7 access, priority support, exclusive hours
- **Status:** Special designation, public recognition, badges, preferred status
- **Duration:** Extended engagement — 6 months to a year
- **Speed:** First in line, priority processing, <10 min response time
- **Experience:** White glove treatment, personal cell access, nice gifts, concierge
- **Access:** In-person retreat, behind-the-scenes, normally restricted areas
- **Network:** Connection to other high-value customers
- **Secrets:** Exclusive bonuses, trade secrets, vetted vendor access

**Structure:** 1–3 core offer components + 3 bonuses (revealed sequentially during the launch to maintain momentum).

---

## How Much to Charge

Take your average transaction size. Add a zero. That's a starting point. The goal is 10x–50x your normal price.

Examples:
- Gym member at $150/month → Fast Cash offer at $1,500–$7,500
- Agency client at $3,000/month → Fast Cash offer at $30,000–$150,000
- Course customer at $500 → Fast Cash offer at $5,000–$25,000

Price so high that it self-selects for buyers with real purchasing power. The value must match — you will work more for these customers. That's the trade.

---

## How Many to Sell

Cap it at 5–10% of your customer base (or at fulfillment capacity). Selling out creates:
- Social proof for future runs
- Urgency during the window
- Waitlist of disappointed buyers who buy faster next time

## 7-Day Launch Sequence (Push to Consult)

**Day 7:** Tease text — "something big coming tomorrow"

**Day 6:** Announcement email + text
- List 1–3 core components
- Include price range ("four-figure investment" or "must be willing to invest $X")
- CTA: book a consultation

**Day 5:** Recap email
- Bullet points from Day 6 (for people who missed it)
- Drop Bonus 1 (e.g., status bonus — special access/recognition)

**Day 3:** Sales update
- How many spots filled so far
- How many left
- Remind them of dream outcome + nightmare avoided
- CTA: book now, first come first served

**Day 2:** Scarcity email + text
- X spots remaining
- Drop Bonus 2 (e.g., special training or resource)
- Reiterate benefits

**Day 1 AM:** Final warning — spots closing soon
- Drop your best bonus (should be worth more than the entire ticket price)
- This closes out anyone still on the fence

**Day 1 PM:** Sold out announcement
- Confirms the promo was real
- Creates FOMO for the next round

**(Optional) Day 2 post-close:** One spot opened up (if you had a cancellation)
- Catches anyone who missed it by hours

## 5-Day Sequence (Push to Automated Checkout)

Use this when you have a large list and can sell without a sales call.

**Day 5:** Primer text — something big coming tomorrow

**Day 4:** Announcement email + text
- 1–3 core components
- Cart opens in 48 hours

**Day 2:** Cart countdown email
- Reminder it's first come first served
- Get them to set an alarm

**Day 1 AM:** Drop Bonus 1, remind them to have card ready

**Day 1 (60 min before cart):** Text reminder

**Day 1 (cart open):** Drop best bonus immediately, cart opens

**Day 1 +1hr:** Sales update (X sold, Y left)

**Day 1 +6hr:** X spots remaining + drop one final bonus

**Day 1 +12hr:** Sold out

## 5 Reasons to Run Every 90 Days

1. **Irregular to customers, predictable to you** — they don't know when it's coming, so urgency is genuine
2. **Revenue floor** — even in slow months, you know a fast cash event is 4 weeks away
3. **Tests new offers** — each run reveals what the market will pay for
4. **Sells out your back catalog** — past customers who never bought a follow-up offer can be converted
5. **Profit leverage** — Fast Cash is typically 15% of annual revenue but ~50% of profit

## Ultra-Premium Example Offers

**B2C — Fitness ($10,000):**
- 1-on-1 year-long "Total Transformation" package
- Core: tailored supplements, meals, workouts, photoshoot
- Bonus 1: Year-long custom nutrition plan + meal prep company intro
- Bonus 2: 3x/week 1-on-1 training sessions with trainer
- Bonus 3: 24/7 personal cell phone access

**B2B — Marketing Agency ($50,000/year):**
- Personal brand explosion package
- Core: script, film, edit, post all content, 4x/year
- Bonus 1: Additional social platform management
- Bonus 2: Lead magnet creation
- Bonus 3: Organic funnel with video sales letters

**Recurring — Any Business (monthly price × 12):**
- Annual "1 Year to [Ultimate Result]" upgrade
- Bonus 1: Custom work on their specific challenge
- Bonus 2: Faster-results mechanism
- Bonus 3: Near-guarantee mechanism

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Running it ad-hoc when in crisis | Schedule it every 90 days regardless of cash position |
| Setting price too low | Start at 10x your normal price — that's the floor |
| Not capping spots | Scarcity is required. Unlimited spots kills urgency |
| Dropping all bonuses at once | Release bonuses sequentially to maintain momentum through the launch |
| Skipping the sold-out message | Announcing sold-out proves scarcity and primes the next run |
