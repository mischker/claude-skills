# Decoy Offer — Attraction Offer

## Overview

Advertise a free or deeply discounted "decoy." When the lead shows interest, present the premium offer alongside it. The decoy makes the premium look like the obvious choice.

**Core mechanic:** People self-select. The decoy attracts leads. The premium offer converts them. You lose nothing by offering both.

## Structure

**Step 1: Design the decoy**
- Free DIY option, free plan, free resource, free call
- Must solve a real problem — not a teaser, a genuine useful thing
- The decoy is what you advertise; not the premium

**Step 2: Design the premium**
- Same outcome as decoy, but faster / easier / done-for-them
- Premium = Done-For-You or Done-With-You version of the decoy's Done-By-You
- Price the premium at 5–20x the decoy's value (or vs. $0 decoy)

**Step 3: Present both at the moment of interest**
- When lead says "tell me more" or shows up for the free thing → show the contrast
- Assumed close: present as "most people do X (premium)" not "would you like X?"

## The Contrast Presentation

> "You've got two options. Option A: you take everything we just walked through and do it yourself — it's free, and I can send you the resources now. Option B: we do it with you / for you over 8 weeks — that's [price]. Most people who are serious about [outcome] go with Option B because [reason]. Which makes more sense for you?"

The contrast makes Option B's price feel reasonable against Option A's effort cost.

## Making the Contrast Huge

The bigger the gap between decoy and premium, the easier the close:

| Decoy | Premium | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| 47-page PDF | 12-week group program | Effort saved |
| Free 30-min call | Done-for-you campaign | Time saved |
| Free plan (limited) | Full plan (unlimited) | Access |
| Video course | Live implementation | Accountability |

## Assumed Close Language

Instead of: "Would you like the premium option?"
Say: "So you're gonna want Option B — which works better for you, [payment A] or [payment B]?"

Assume they're buying. Only walk it back if they explicitly say no.

## Important Notes

- Advertise the decoy, not the premium (the decoy generates leads)
- The free thing must be genuinely useful — don't bait-and-switch
- Gym Launch example: free courses → premium $16,000 licensing → $476K/month in 3 months
- You can use this offer structure in cold ads, on a website, or in a sales call

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Decoy is a fake / terrible | Make decoy genuinely good — it builds trust |
| Advertising premium directly | Lead with decoy; premium only after interest shown |
| Not presenting both simultaneously | Contrast is the mechanism — show both |
| Soft language on premium | Use assumed close; they expect guidance |
