# Affiliate Program — Build An Army That Advertises For You

## Overview

Affiliates are independent businesses that tell their audience to buy your stuff in exchange for commissions. Unlike referrals (customers sending friends), affiliates are businesses with their own audiences who do the core four on your behalf.

**Why affiliates:**
- Every affiliate you get adds a compounding stream of leads
- You sell once, they send leads forever (if you integrate well)
- Scalable to any size — super-affiliates bring affiliates who bring more affiliates

**Leverage math:**
- 10 affiliates → each brings 1 customer/month → +$100K/month added revenue
- In 12 months: $7.8M vs. $1.2M selling customers directly (same effort)

**Prestige Labs example:** $150K in first 3 weeks → $450K+ in week 4 → averaged $400K+/week after. No paid ads. No sales team. Affiliates did everything.

## 6-Step Process

### Step 1: Find Your Ideal Affiliates

The ideal affiliate has a warm audience already full of your ideal customers.

**Answer these questions about your best customers:**
- What do they buy? → Who provides that?
- Where do they go? → What businesses are nearby?
- What do they like to do? → Who provides those services?
- Where do they work? → What companies employ them?

**Key question: "Who's got my leads?"**

**Organize by category:**
- Software they use
- Products they buy
- Services they use
- Groups they belong to
- Events they attend
- Publications they read

Businesses that fall into multiple categories = highest potential affiliates. Build a hit list.

**ALAN example:** ALAN's ideal affiliates were digital agencies. So ALAN listed 200 products/services for agencies. Agency software, tools, training programs, events — all potential affiliate partners.

---

### Step 2: Make Them an Offer

Advertise your affiliate offer the same way you advertise anything — callout + value + CTA. But the offer is different: instead of your core product, you're offering a **new revenue stream**.

**Affiliate value equation:**
> "Make more money from your current customers and get more leads than your current offer can, because your customers already want this product — without building, delivering, or supporting the product yourself — starting tomorrow."

**Callout options:**
- The affiliate owner: "ATTENTION SPA OWNERS…"
- Their customers: "Do you work with busy professionals?"
- Results they promise: "To the people who transform others' health…"
- Products they sell: "If you sell essential oils, this is for you…"
- Your own customers: "Do you know anyone who owns a spa?"

---

### Step 3: Qualify Them

Turn potential affiliates into actual ones by getting them invested.

**Make them a customer:** Require affiliates to buy and use your product. If they don't believe in it enough to buy it, they shouldn't sell it. Buyers become better sellers.

**Make them an expert:** Charge for onboarding and certification. Certified affiliates are more motivated (they invested), more capable (they learned), and more credible (they can speak from experience).

**How much to charge for onboarding:**
> 10–20% of what the average active affiliate makes in the first 12 months.

Example: Average affiliate makes $40K/year → charge $4K–$8K for onboarding. Too low = no skin in the game. Too high = not enough affiliates.

**If not enough affiliates sign up:** Lower the commitment.
**If not enough follow through:** Raise it.

---

### Step 4: Figure Out What to Pay Them

**What to pay for:** New customers (primary). Repeat customers (ongoing). Advanced: pay per lead, booked call, or other conversion steps you know reliably lead to sales.

**How much to pay:**
Base payout on your **maximum allowable CAC** (1/3 of LTGP for a 3:1 ratio).

**Three-tier payout structure** (using max CAC = $40 as example):
| Tier | Payout | Qualification |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | 25% CAC = $10 | Anyone who signs up and buys the product/certification |
| Tier 2 | 50% CAC = $20 | Completes onboarding + does first launch or specific number of posts |
| Tier 3 | 100% CAC = $40 | Sustains 5+ customers/month |

**Why tiered payouts work:**
- Average payout is much less than your max CAC (since most are tier 1–2)
- Extra savings fund contests, recruiting new affiliates, rewarding top performers
- Example: 20% tier 1 + 20% tier 2 + 60% tier 3 = blended payout of $30 vs. max of $40 → LTGP:CAC improves from 3:1 to 4:1

**Never cap payouts** — top affiliates drive compounding growth.

---

### Step 5: Get Them Advertising — Launch

The best way to activate new affiliates: a **big launch with a clear payoff, fast**.

Use the **Whisper → Tease → Shout** method:

**Whisper (curiosity phase):**
- Start 2–4 months out; hint at something big coming
- Show behind-the-scenes of the product being built
- Embed questions — "not yet" is your best friend
- Frequency: every 4–6 weeks until 60 days out, then every 2–3 weeks until 30 days out

**Tease (reveal phase):**
- Reveal the product and launch date
- Start showing value elements (What-Who-When from `paid-ads`)
- Show results, testimonials, specifics
- Frequency: once/week until 14 days out, twice/week until 3 days out

**Shout (action phase):**
- Specific CTAs — what to do when the product launches
- Bonuses for early buyers, scarcity, urgency
- Frequency: twice/day from 3 days out → every few hours on launch day → every 30 minutes the last 2 hours

**For affiliates specifically:** Give them the content and materials they need to do the whisper-tease-shout for you. They plug in and play. You get the leads.

---

### Step 6: Keep Them Advertising — Integration

Getting affiliates to launch once is the start. Making them a permanent lead source is the goal.

**Three levels of integration (easiest to hardest):**

**Level 1: Affiliates give away your lead magnet with every purchase**
Your lead magnet makes their offer more valuable → they can charge more → you get leads → you upsell your core offer.

Examples:
- Massage studio offers free massage with every personal training package (studio benefits, you get massage leads)
- Chiropractor offers free posture assessment with every gym membership (gym gets stronger offer, chiropractor gets patients)
- Supplement company offers free nutrition consult with every gym membership

**Level 2: Affiliates sell your lead magnet separately**
Affiliates keep 100% of the lead magnet revenue. You get the lead. You upsell your core offer. Affiliates love this because it's pure profit for them.

**Level 3: Affiliates sell your core offer**
They become resellers. They earn a commission on your core offer. Your deliver; they sell. This is the highest-revenue option but requires more training and trust.

**Which to choose:** Test all three. Hormozi uses Level 1 (twice/year as a big event) and Level 3 (ongoing). Level 2 works well in other portfolio companies.

## 3 Case Studies

**Case Study 1 — Services (LLC Formation):**
- Core offer: LLC setup, bookkeeping, tax prep
- Affiliates: business training programs (already teach new entrepreneurs)
- Lead magnet: free LLC setup given with every training program purchase
- Integration: free LLC → sales call → sell bookkeeping/tax services
- Result: $50M business, zero paid ads

**Case Study 2 — Physical Products (Prestige Labs supplements):**
- Core offer: supplements for gym members
- Affiliates: 4,500+ gym owners (already sell memberships)
- Launch: free 28-day challenge with supplements included
- Integration: gym owners do nutrition orientations for every new member → sell supplements
- Result: 4,000 gyms × 14 sales/month × $200 average order = millions/month

**Case Study 3 — Local Service (Chiropractor):**
- Core offer: chiropractic adjustments
- Affiliates: gym owners
- Launch: 3-hour workshop on posture/exercise at the gym; gym owner keeps ticket revenue
- Integration: chiropractor included in every new gym membership (1–2 free adjustments)
- Result: Repeat with 30 gyms → more patients than they can handle

## ROI Calculation

Cost to get one affiliate ÷ total gross profit from all customers they send = affiliate LTGP:CAC

**Example:**
- Cost to acquire affiliate: $4,000 (advertising)
- Affiliate sells $10K/month × 12 months = $120K total sales
- 75% gross margin → $90K gross profit
- Pay affiliate 40%: $36K payout
- Leftover: $54K

**Affiliate LTGP:CAC = $54K / $4K = 13.5:1**

If ratio is below 3:1:
1. Lower affiliate CAC (better ads, offer, sales)
2. Increase activation rate (better launch process)
3. Increase LTGP (better integration strategy)

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Recruiting affiliates who don't use the product | Make them a customer first — believers sell better |
| Free or cheap onboarding | Charge 10–20% of year-1 affiliate earnings; paid affiliates follow through |
| One big launch, no integration | Launch gets them started; integration keeps them going forever |
| Capping payouts | Never cap — top affiliates compound; capping kills motivation |
| Starting with core offer instead of lead magnet | Lead magnet integration is easiest and creates the most goodwill |
