# Value Multiplier

## Overview

Value has two components: perceived value (what the buyer believes they'll get) and economic value (what the buyer produces for your business over their lifetime). Both can be multiplied dramatically without changing your core product.

**Hormozi's hierarchy of levers — highest leverage first:**
1. Raise prices
2. Increase customer lifetime value (Crazy Eight)
3. Increase perceived value of the offer (Value Equation)
4. Optimize avatar (serve only the best customers)

Work top-down. Don't add more customers to a leaky bucket.

---

## Lever 1: Pricing — The Highest Leverage Move

**Doubling price with the same customers = 6× profit (not 2×).** Every other lever only achieves 3.5×.

Why: Fixed costs (delivery, acquisition) don't change when you raise price. All extra revenue drops to the bottom line.

### Three pricing models (ranked by effectiveness)
1. **Value-based** — price anchored to the outcome's value to the buyer ✓ Use this
2. **Competitor-based** — price anchored to what others charge ✗ Race to the bottom
3. **Cost-plus** — price anchored to your costs ✗ Leaves most profit on the table

### How to find the right price
- Start selling, then raise price 20% every 10 sales
- Track: conversion rate × lifetime gross profit = profit-maximizing price
- Stop when conversion drops dramatically; go back one increment
- Test pricing every quarter — companies that do make more profit and grow faster

### Quick pricing plays
| Play | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| Monthly → 28-day billing | Bills 13× per year instead of 12 (~8% lift) |
| Annual billing option | Collect 12 months upfront, offer 10-15% discount |
| Annual price increase | 5-10% raise per year; lock in existing, new rate for new |
| Ultra-high-ticket anchor | Create a 10× tier to make core offer feel affordable |
| Guarantee upsell | Charge separately for extended guarantee/warranty |
| 10× the 10% | Get 10% of customers to pay 10× current price = double revenue |

## Lever 2: Lifetime Value — The Crazy Eight

Eight ways to make each customer worth more (in order of activation ease):

| # | Move | How |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Raise prices** | Same delivery, more revenue — highest leverage |
| 2 | **Lower delivery costs** | Same price, better margins |
| 3 | **Upsell Frequency** | Get them to buy again later (continuity, subscription) |
| 4 | **Upsell Quantity** | Get them to buy more in one transaction (bundles) |
| 5 | **Upsell Quality** | Get them to buy the premium version (DFY > DWY > DIY) |
| 6 | **Downsell Quantity** | Offer fewer units rather than lose the sale entirely |
| 7 | **Downsell Quality** | Offer a lower tier rather than lose the sale entirely |
| 8 | **Cross-sell** | Sell something adjacent that solves their next problem |

**The best upsell formula (Hormozi):** More of what they just bought — with faster results, less risk, less effort, less hassle — for more money.

## Lever 3: Value Equation Manipulation

Increase perceived value by pulling each of the four drivers:

### Increase Dream Outcome
- Make the outcome more specific and time-bound
- Connect it to the buyer's status or identity
- Sell the destination, not the vehicle ("Greek god physique in 6 months" vs. "fitness program")

### Increase Perceived Likelihood
- Add testimonials from buyers who look like the current prospect
- Show the mechanism — why it works, not just that it works
- Add a guarantee that eliminates purchase risk
- Show number of customers, before/after data, named case studies

### Decrease Time to Value
- Identify the first win achievable in <60 minutes or day 1
- Build that quick win into onboarding/kickoff
- Offer a DWY kickoff call for immediate momentum
- Remove all setup friction before the first value moment

### Decrease Effort & Sacrifice
- Upgrade DIY → DWY → DFY wherever margin allows
- Add templates, checklists, swipe files — tools over training
- Handle the steps customers hate most (admin, setup, integrations)
- Automate recurring manual tasks

## Lever 4: Delivery Vehicle Upgrade

The same outcome, delivered at a higher tier of service, commands dramatically more money:

| Tier | What it is | Price multiple |
|---|---|---|
| DIY | Course, guide, template — they do it | 1× |
| DWY | Coaching, cohort, workshop — you guide them | 3-5× |
| DFY | Agency, done-for-you — you do it | 10-20× |

**Additional dimensions for upgrade:**

| Dimension | Low → High |
|---|---|
| Scope | Individual → Team → Organization → Market |
| Speed | Standard → Priority → Same-day → Real-time |
| Access | No access → Office hours → 1-on-1 → Personal cell |
| Exclusivity | Public → Cohort-limited → Invite-only |

Each step along any dimension justifies a meaningful price increase.

## Lever 5: Avatar Optimization

Serving better customers is more valuable than serving more customers.

**Hormozi's Vista Method:**
1. Survey all current customers
2. Sort by LTV (spend × retention × gross margin)
3. Focus on top 20% — find 3-5 traits they share
4. Cut channels bringing low-LTV customers
5. Double down on channels and messaging that attract high-LTV customers

**Why it matters:** Hormozi competitor: $6-8k LTV. Hormozi: $45k LTV. Same market. Different avatar targeting. The LTV gap compounds into exponentially higher margins, retention, and referral rates.

**78% of top customers consumed at least 2 pieces of long-form content before buying.** Find what your best customers did before buying — then engineer that path for all prospects.

## The 10×/1/10th Test

Run on any offer, product, or delivery method:

**10× question:** "If the customer paid 10× my current price, what would I include that I'm not including now?"
- Usually reveals: DFY elements, personal access, custom work, speed upgrades, status perks, exclusive network

**1/10th question:** "If I had 1/10th the resources, how would I still deliver the core value?"
- Usually reveals: the minimum viable product — the single thing that delivers 80% of the value

The delta between these two answers is your product ladder: bare MVP at the bottom, ultra-premium at the top.

## Quick Reference: Which Lever to Pull First

| Situation | Pull this lever first |
|---|---|
| Margins are thin | Raise prices |
| Customers churn quickly | LTV: Upsell frequency / reduce churn |
| Good product, low conversion | Offer review (guarantee, proof, dream outcome) |
| Good product, great conversion | Add premium tier (10× price, DFY delivery) |
| Customers are low-quality / high-churn | Avatar optimization — cut bad-fit segments |
| Offer is commoditized | Delivery vehicle upgrade (DIY → DFY) |
| Revenue plateau | 10× the 10% fast cash play to existing customers |
