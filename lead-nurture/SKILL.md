# Lead Nurture — Maximize Show Rates

## Overview

Getting leads is not the same as getting customers. The bridge between the two is show rate: the percentage of leads who actually show up to the appointment or sales conversation.

**The math:**
```
100 leads × 50% schedule rate = 50 appointments
50 appointments × 50% show rate = 25 shown
Throughput: 25% (from 100 leads to 25 actual sales opportunities)
```

A 20–40% increase in show rate = 2–3x profit at 20% margins. No new advertising required.

**The problem:** Most businesses don't act on this because they don't see no-shows as lost money. Start treating every no-show as a lost sale — it changes how much effort you put in.

---

## The Four Pillars

### Pillar 1: Availability

**If leads can't schedule at the time they want, they schedule with someone else.**

The data: The business with the most available appointment times wins. Not the best offer. Not the best price. Availability.

Actions:
- Extend hours to evenings and weekends
- Add more time slots — particularly same-day and next-day
- Allow online booking 24/7 (not just during business hours)
- Accept walk-ins when possible (removes all friction)
- Reduce time-to-next-available-appointment as a core metric

The closer the available slot is to right now, the higher the show rate.

### Pillar 2: Speed to Contact

**Call leads within 5 minutes. The average business takes 42 hours. That's your competitive advantage.**

From InsideSales.com data: calling within 5 minutes vs. 30 minutes = 100x higher contact rate.

Speed rules:
1. **First contact target:** ≤5 minutes from opt-in for high-ticket, ≤24 hours minimum for any business
2. **First appointment target:** ≤48 hours from first contact. The further out the appointment, the lower the show rate
3. Automate the first touch (text/email) to go out immediately, even if the human call follows
4. For any booked appointment >2 days out: add at least 2 reminder touchpoints

### Pillar 3: Personalization

**Generic follow-up gets ignored. Personal follow-up gets responses. The more responses, the more shows.**

Personalization levels:
- **Level 1:** Use their name, reference what they opted in for
- **Level 2:** Reference specific details from their form/survey answers
- **Level 3:** Reference their business/situation specifically (requires research)
- **Level 4:** Frame every follow-up message in terms of *their* goal, not your product

**The personalization-response loop:** More personalized messages → higher response rates → higher show rates. When a lead responds at all, they are 3x more likely to show up.

Script structure for follow-up messages:
```
[Name] — [specific reference to their situation] — [one sentence on what they stand to gain from showing up] — [CTA: confirm time or reschedule if needed]
```

### Pillar 4: Volume

**Most salespeople give up after 1.3 attempts. Optimal is 5+.**

Industry data: 44% of salespeople stop after the first attempt. 80% of sales require 5+ contacts.

Follow-up sequence (from first opt-in):
- Immediately: automated text/email
- Same day: phone call
- Day 2: text or email
- Day 4: phone call + email
- Day 7: final "closing the loop" message

**The closing loop message:** "Hey [Name], I've tried to reach you a few times. I'm going to close out your file unless I hear from you — totally understand if the timing isn't right. If you want to pick this back up just reply to this or call [number]."

This message alone recovers 10–20% of unresponsive leads.

**For no-shows:** Have a same-day no-show protocol:
1. Call immediately after missed appointment
2. Text 30 minutes later
3. Email that evening
4. Follow-up call next morning

## Execution Checklist

**Setup (one-time):**
- [ ] Automated immediate text/email response for all opt-ins
- [ ] Online booking link available 24/7
- [ ] Appointment reminders set for 24 hours before and 1 hour before
- [ ] Defined no-show protocol with specific timing
- [ ] Scripts for each follow-up touchpoint written and assigned

**Ongoing:**
- [ ] Measure schedule rate weekly (scheduled ÷ engaged leads)
- [ ] Measure show rate weekly (shown ÷ scheduled)
- [ ] Measure throughput monthly (shown ÷ total leads)
- [ ] Identify which sources have the highest/lowest show rates
- [ ] A/B test follow-up message personalization level

## Benchmarks by Business Type

| Business | Good Show Rate | Great Show Rate |
|---|---|---|
| B2B sales | 50–60% | 70%+ |
| Local service (fitness, medical) | 60–70% | 80%+ |
| High-ticket coaching | 60–70% | 80%+ |
| B2C general | 40–50% | 60%+ |

If you're below benchmark: start with Pillar 2 (speed). The single biggest lever in most businesses is response time.

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Calling leads next-day or later | Call within 5 minutes for any lead from paid ads |
| Scheduling appointments 5+ days out | Keep first appointments within 48 hours of contact |
| Sending generic reminder messages | Add one personal detail; response rate 2–3x improves |
| Giving up after 1–2 follow-up attempts | 5–8 follow-ups is normal for non-responsive leads |
| Treating no-shows as lost | 30–40% of no-shows will reschedule if you follow up immediately |
