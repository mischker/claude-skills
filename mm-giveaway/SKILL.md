# Giveaway — Attraction Offer

## Overview

Advertise a grand prize giveaway. Collect contact info to enter. Pick one winner. Offer everyone else the prize (or similar) at a discount for a limited time.

**Core mechanic:** The grand prize creates desire. The promotional offer monetizes everyone who didn't win.

## Structure

**Step 1: Design the grand prize**
- Must be aspirational to your target avatar
- High perceived value (the bigger the better)
- Related to your core offer so it attracts the right leads

**Step 2: Run the giveaway**
- Advertise the prize, not your business
- Collect: name, email, phone (and anything else you want)
- Add a referral mechanic: "Enter twice if you refer a friend" → dual-prize doubles leads
- Consult legal in your jurisdiction — giveaway laws vary

**Step 3: Pick a winner**
- Announce publicly (social proof, credibility)
- Contact non-winners immediately after

**Step 4: Convert non-winners**
- Make a promotional offer to everyone who entered but didn't win
- 7-day deadline — urgency is real, use it
- Offer: significant discount + bonus, or the grand prize experience at a cost

## The Dual-Prize Referral Mechanic

> "Enter once for yourself. Share with a friend and you both get an entry. If either of you wins, you both get a prize."

This doubles lead volume for zero extra ad spend.

## The Non-Winner Offer

Send within 24 hours of winner announcement:

> "We only had one grand prize, but we don't want you to leave empty-handed. For the next 7 days, you can get [similar prize / discounted offer] — this is only for giveaway participants. [CTA]"

Key: Make the offer feel exclusive to participants, not a general discount.

## What to Offer Non-Winners

| Business type | Grand prize | Non-winner offer |
|---|---|---|
| Fitness | 6-month coaching free | 6-week challenge at 50% off |
| SaaS | 1-year subscription free | 3 months at 60% off |
| E-commerce | $500 product bundle | $150 order for $75 |
| Consulting | Done-for-you project | Done-with-you workshop |

## Important Notes

- Keep the 7-day deadline hard — don't extend it
- Legal review required before running (no purchase necessary rules, disclosures)
- Track entry source to measure ad ROI
- Giveaways work best as list builders — the promotional offer is where money is made

## After the 7-Day Deadline

If non-winners don't take the promotional offer:
- Day 8: Send one final reminder ("Last chance" subject line — be direct, not pushy)
- Beyond Day 8: Move them into your standard email nurture sequence
- 90-day nurture window: typically converts 15–20% of missed non-winner offers at full price

Don't discount again in the nurture. Urgency was real and gone — value-led content from here.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Prize irrelevant to offer → wrong leads | Prize must relate directly to core business |
| No follow-up offer → just built a list | Non-winner offer is the whole point |
| Deadline too long → urgency collapses | 7 days maximum |
| Legal compliance skipped | Consult attorney before running |
