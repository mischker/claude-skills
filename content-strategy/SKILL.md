# Content Strategy — Build An Audience That Buys

## Overview

Free content grows your warm audience, which makes all other advertising more efficient. The content you create isn't the compounding asset — **the audience is**. Even when specific content disappears, the audience keeps growing.

**Key finding:** 78% of Gym Launch customers consumed 2+ long-form pieces of content before booking a call. Free content was quietly doing the selling.

---

## The Content Unit (All Content Is Made of These)

Every piece of content has three jobs:

**1. Hook** — Get their attention

**2. Retain** — Keep their attention  

**3. Reward** — Satisfy why they stopped

There's no such thing as too long, only too boring. The same person who skips a 10-second video will binge a 900-page book if it keeps rewarding them.

---

## Hook: Getting Attention

People scan dozens of options. Your hook competes with everything else. "After writing your headline, you've spent 80 cents of your advertising dollar." — David Ogilvy

**Topics that hook:**
| Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Far past** | Old lessons with wisdom to share | "The belief I held for 5 years that kept me broke" |
| **Recent past** | What happened last week | "A founder I spoke with yesterday said something that stopped me cold" |
| **Present** | Ideas as they occur — post them immediately | Tweet ideas in real time |
| **Trending** | Apply your expertise to what's happening now | Your take on a viral story in your industry |
| **Manufactured** | Create the experience to talk about | "I lived on $100 for a month — here's what I learned" |

**7 headline components** (include at least 2):
- **Recency** — as recent as possible
- **Relevancy** — personally meaningful to your avatar
- **Celebrity** — includes someone prominent
- **Proximity** — geographically close ("Chicago Business Owners")
- **Conflict** — opposing ideas or people
- **Unusual** — rare, bizarre, out of the ordinary
- **Ongoing** — story in progress with unknown outcome

**Verbal callouts:**
- Labels: "Gym Owners…" / "Remote Workers…" / "Moms of toddlers…"
- Yes-Questions: "Do you wake up exhausted no matter how much you sleep?"
- If-Then: "If you run over $100k/month in ads, this is for you…"
- Ridiculous results: "This gym owner booked out 2 years in advance — clients are furious"

**Non-verbal callouts:**
- Contrast: bright colors, movement, attractive people
- Likeness: look like your ideal customer
- Scene: visually show the problem (tossing and turning = sleep issues)

### Step from "How to" to "How I"

"How I built a 7-figure agency" > "How to build a 7-figure agency"

When you talk about your experience, no one can question it. This makes you bulletproof to critics and more credible to buyers.

## Retain: Keeping Attention

Use **curiosity** — embed unanswered questions. Three formats:

**Lists:** "7 ways I invested $1000 in my 20s that paid off big" — the number tells them what's coming, so they stay.

**Steps:** "3 steps to creating a great hook" — sequential order + clear goal = built-in retention.

**Stories:** Start with conflict or uncertainty. People stay to find out what happens next.

Combine: steps within stories, lists within steps, stories to prove each list item. Complexity adds retention.

---

## Reward: Delivering Value

Content is only as good as how often it keeps its promise. Good reward means:
- Delivering exactly what the hook promised
- Answering all the questions you embedded
- Providing value that matches or exceeds their expectation

If your audience isn't growing, your content isn't rewarding well enough. Get better by doing more and studying feedback.

## The Give:Ask Ratio

| Platform | Give:Ask ratio |
|---|---|
| Television (mature) | 3.5:1 |
| Facebook feed (mature) | 4:1 |
| Growing platforms | Give give give… maybe ask |
| Best strategy | **Give until they ask** |

**"Give until they ask"** — when you give enough, people feel uncomfortable not reciprocating. They'll DM you, email you, go to your website. These are your best customers — they feel they still get the better end of the deal even as paying customers.

**The moment you start asking is the moment growth slows.** The more patient you are, the more you get when you finally ask.

---

## How to Ask (When You Have To)

Two strategies:

**Integrated:** Small CTA embedded in every piece of content. Works best on long-form (podcasts, videos, articles). Keep the ask under 3% of total runtime.

**Intermittent:** Make 10 "give" posts, then 1 "ask" post. Works best on short-form platforms.

**What to advertise:**
1. **Lead magnet** — lower risk, gets more people into your world
2. **Core offer** — direct path to revenue

**Lead magnet CTA example:**
> "I have 11 more tactics on this. Go to [site] to grab the visual."

**Core offer CTA example:**
> "I'm looking for 5 [avatar] to help them [dream outcome] in [timeframe] without [effort]. If they don't get [result], I'll give money back AND keep working. If that sounds fair, DM me."

## 7 Content Lessons

1. **"How I" beats "How to"** — experience can't be questioned
2. **Repeat yourself** — 1 in 5 who see your post don't know you have an offer; say it again
3. **Puddles → Ponds → Oceans** — narrow content for a specific niche first; expand when you've won that niche
4. **Create a "greatest hits" list** — label each by the problem it solves; give to sales team
5. **Free content retains paying customers** — customers include free content in their ROI calculation
6. **No short attention spans, only higher standards** — make it good, not short
7. **Post manually, not scheduled** — manual posting gives you one last pressure to improve before publishing

---

## Scaling: Two Strategies

**Depth then width:** Maximize one platform before adding another.
- Advantages: compound faster, fewer resources needed
- Disadvantages: single-platform risk, slower reach

**Width then depth:** Get on every platform early, then maximize.
- Advantages: broader reach, content repurposing efficiency
- Disadvantages: more labor, risk of spreading thin

**Repurposing rule:** The same short video takes minimal extra effort to format for multiple platforms. Text to LinkedIn + Twitter costs almost nothing extra.

**Scale steps (for each platform):**
1. Post consistently
2. Maximize quality + quantity on that platform
3. Add next platform while maintaining the first

---

## Benchmarks

Track monthly:
- **Total followers across all platforms** (how big)
- **Growth rate**: (new followers / starting followers) × 100 (how fast)

Audience size compounds — same growth rate on a larger base = faster absolute growth. Be consistent for at least 6 months before judging a platform.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Skipping content because "no one follows me" | You start at zero. Post anyway. Audience compounds. |
| Asking too much, too soon | Give until they ask — over-asking slows growth |
| Trying to be on every platform at once with no quality | Pick one, master it, then expand |
| Judging a platform after 1–3 months | Give it at least 6 months of consistent posting |
| Pre-scheduling everything | Post manually for one final push on quality |
| Making it about you | Make it about the result the audience wants |
