# Marketing Machine — Customer-Generated Ad System

## Overview

A business where all advertising depends on the founder is unsellable and fragile. The Marketing Machine is a system that turns normal business operations into a continuous stream of ad-quality video, testimonials, and social proof — without the founder doing anything extra.

**The insight:** The bottom of your activity list (customer calls, events, awards, support) feeds the top (advertising). You're already creating content. You just don't have a system to capture and use it.

**The result:** 80% of Gym Launch's top-performing ads featured customers — not Hormozi. Member-generated content outperformed founder-recorded ads. And it allowed the business to sell for $46.2M.

---

## The 7 Nodes

Implement these as a checklist. Each checked node = a reliable stream of ad-quality content. Start with whichever is easiest for your business. One is better than none.

### Node 1: Lifecycle Ads

**What it is:** Clips from real customer interactions at each stage of their journey — "before" (sales calls), "during" (support/coaching calls), and "after" (upsell, milestone, or testimonial calls).

**Why it works:** Shows the customer's actual emotional arc with your business. "Before I was struggling with X... now I'm doing Y." You're showing, not telling.

**Setup checklist:**
- [ ] Add consent language to all customer contracts (permission to use recordings for advertising)
- [ ] Record all calls: sales, onboarding, support, ascension, milestone
- [ ] Add extra storage — turn off auto-delete
- [ ] Create calendar events for all calls with customer name in the title
- [ ] Sales team sends "happy customer calls" to marketing weekly
- [ ] Marketing team clips: (1) before moment from sales call, (2) during moment from support call, (3) after moment from upsell/milestone call
- [ ] Create a labeling system so clips are findable at a moment's notice

### Node 2: Social Media Scrape

**What it is:** Mining all platforms where your customers already post about you — reviews, tags, comments, mentions — for content you can repurpose.

**Setup:**
- [ ] Set up Google Alerts and platform mentions for your brand name
- [ ] Monitor review sites (Google, Yelp, G2, Trustpilot, etc.) weekly
- [ ] Screenshot and save every positive post/comment customers make
- [ ] Ask permission to repost (adds authenticity)
- [ ] Create a folder/database organized by type: result, emotion, recommendation

### Node 3: In-Person Events

**What it is:** Capturing raw customer reactions at physical moments — delivery moments, reveals, award ceremonies, results reveals.

**Examples:**
- Kitchen remodel completion: customer walks in with eyes closed
- Weight loss progress: stepping on scale after 30 days
- Dental work reveal: seeing results in the mirror
- "You got the job!" reaction for a recruiting firm
- Award ceremony for high-achieving customers

**Setup:**
- [ ] Identify the 1–3 emotional delivery moments in your business
- [ ] Assign someone to film those moments (designated role)
- [ ] Get consent at time of filming
- [ ] Edit: reaction clips become hooks (first 3–5 seconds), full clips become ads

### Node 4: Communications & Communities Scrape

**What it is:** Mining group chats, Slack communities, email replies, forums, and DMs for authentic customer success stories and language.

**Setup:**
- [ ] Identify all channels where customers communicate with you or each other
- [ ] Set up weekly scan of those channels for wins, breakthroughs, expressions of gratitude
- [ ] Screenshot and save with permission
- [ ] Build a searchable archive of customer language — their words become your headlines

### Node 5: Bonuses, Milestones & Awards

**What it is:** Celebrating customer wins publicly and capturing the content of those moments.

**Setup:**
- [ ] Create a milestone recognition system (first lead, first $10K, first year, etc.)
- [ ] Hold regular award events or virtual ceremonies
- [ ] Film the award moments — customer reactions, acceptance "speeches"
- [ ] Post to social with customer permission
- [ ] Use award content as social proof in ads

### Node 6: Competitions

**What it is:** Running challenges or competitions where customers create content (transformations, results, stories) as part of competing — you get the content, they get recognition.

**Examples:**
- "30-day transformation challenge" with photos
- "Results competition" with video testimonials
- "Referral contest" where customers create invite content

**Setup:**
- [ ] Define the competition structure and prize
- [ ] Require video/photo submissions as entry
- [ ] Get content rights in the submission form
- [ ] Repurpose submissions as ads and organic content

### Node 7: Delivery Content

**What it is:** Documenting the delivery of your product or service as it happens — behind-the-scenes, process videos, results in real time.

**Examples:**
- B2B: showing the actual work being done (strategy session, audit, build)
- Physical product: unboxing, first use
- Service: before and after transformation documentation

## Implementation Priority

Start with the node that requires least new infrastructure:

| Node | Best for | Infrastructure needed |
|---|---|---|
| Lifecycle Ads | Any business with calls | Call recording + consent form |
| Milestones & Awards | Coaching, community, B2B | Regular recognition cadence |
| Social Media Scrape | Any business | 30 min/week monitoring |
| In-Person Events | Local service, physical delivery | Camera + designated filmer |
| Communities Scrape | Community, SaaS | 30 min/week monitoring |
| Competitions | B2C, fitness, education | Simple contest framework |
| Delivery Content | Any | Documenting existing work |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Thinking founder-created content is best | 80% of Gym Launch's top ads had zero founder content |
| Waiting to "capture content" at big events only | Moments happen daily on every customer call — system must be always-on |
| Not getting consent | Add permissions to every customer contract; ask verbally at filming |
| Capturing but not organizing | Content you can't find is content you can't use. Build a searchable library |
| Implementing all 7 nodes at once | Pick 1, implement fully, then add the next |
