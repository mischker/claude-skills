# Pricing Strategy — Hormozi Framework

## Overview

Price new offers low enough to get lots of yeses. Use customer feedback to improve. Raise prices until you stop making more money. Never lower price for the same product — change what they get instead.

**The goal:** Find the price where (conversion rate × margin) is maximized — not the highest price, and not the most customers.

---

## Starting Price

**Start low. Get yeses. Improve. Raise.**

1. Price new offer to guarantee yeses — you need reps and feedback more than margin
2. Collect feedback from every customer: what worked, what didn't
3. Fix the product based on feedback
4. Raise the price
5. Repeat until raising price makes you less money than before

**Signal to stop raising:** Revenue goes down after a price increase → you've found your ceiling (for now).

---

## The Four Levers

Every pricing decision moves at least one of these:

| Lever | Effect when increased | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | More revenue per sale | Fewer conversions |
| **Volume** | More total customers | Need more advertising |
| **Margin** | More profit per sale | May require cost cuts |
| **LTV** | More lifetime revenue | Requires retention work |

Don't optimize one lever in isolation. Raising price lowers volume but may raise total revenue. Test, measure, decide.

## Present Price With Interest Baked In

Instead of charging interest on payment plans:

**Wrong:** "It's $1,000. If you do a payment plan, we charge $100 in interest."

**Right:** "It's $1,100. But if you pay in full today, you save $100. That's what most people do."

Same math. The second framing anchors higher and rewards prepayment instead of punishing payment plans.

## Annual vs. Monthly Billing Effect on Churn

(From Profitwell data, 14,000 businesses)

| Billing cadence | Monthly churn |
|---|---|
| Monthly | 10.7% |
| Quarterly | 5% |
| Annual | 2% |

**Default to annual pricing.** Work down to monthly only through the downsell process. More upfront cash AND lower churn.

---

## 4-Week Billing vs. Monthly

Bill every 4 weeks instead of monthly:
- 12 months = 12 payments (monthly)
- 12 months = **13** payments (4-week cycles)
- Difference: **8.3% more revenue** for zero extra work
- At 20% margins: adds 41% to annual profit

Just change "per month" to "every 4 weeks."

---

## Pricing for Continuity vs. One-Time

If you offer both:

| Standalone price (vs. continuity) | % choosing continuity |
|---|---|
| 1.33x higher | 50% |
| 1.66x higher | 60% |
| 2x higher | 70% |
| 2.33x higher | 80% |
| 2.66x higher | 90% |

Set standalone price to hit your desired continuity ratio.

---

## The 3% Processing Fee

> "It's $[X] plus a standard 3% processing fee."

Nobody refuses. At 10% margins, adding 3% = 30% more profit. Use it.

**Pair with:** "Want to save the fee? Give us a second form of payment and we'll waive it." → Gets ACH/backup card, reduces failed payments.

---

## When NOT to Lower Price

Never lower price for the same product just to close a sale. Instead:
- Spread it over time → Payment Plan Downsell
- Remove a feature → Feature Downsell
- Credit a past purchase → Rollover Upsell

Customers talk. One person getting a discount "just because" destroys trust and pricing integrity for everyone.

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Starting too high → no sales data | Start low; get yeses; iterate |
| Never raising prices | Raise until revenue drops, then hold one step below |
| Monthly billing by default | Default to annual; monthly is a downsell |
| Billing monthly not 4-weekly | Switch to 4-week cycles for 8.3% free revenue |
| Discounting same product | Change what they get, not what they pay |
