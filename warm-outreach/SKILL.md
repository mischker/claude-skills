# Warm Outreach — Get Leads From People Who Know You

## Overview

Warm outreach is one-to-one contact with your warm audience: people who gave you permission to contact them. It's the cheapest, most reliable lead source — and most businesses stop doing it too soon.

**When to use:**
- Launching any new offer (validate before spending on ads)
- Starting from zero customers
- Re-engaging an existing list
- Getting your first 5 free customers

**Benchmark:** 100 reach-outs → ~20 replies → ~4 take your free offer → ~1 converts to paid. At $400/offer: 500 reach-outs/week → $104,000/year.

---

## Your List

Everyone has one. Combine:
- Phone contacts
- Email address books (every account you've ever had)
- Social media followers, connections, friends
- Past customers

Most people have 1,000+ contacts across platforms. This is your list.

## 10-Step Process

### Step 1: Get your list
Export contacts from phone, email, LinkedIn, Instagram, etc. Combine into one place.

### Step 2: Pick a platform
Start with the platform where you have the most contacts. Work through all of them over time.

### Step 3: Personalize your opening
Look at their recent posts or profile. Find ONE thing to mention that shows you're a real person.

> "Saw you just got a new puppy — congrats! How's that going?"

### Step 4: Reach out to 100 people per day
Contact each person up to 3 times, once per day for 3 days (or until they respond). After reaching everyone on one platform, move to the next.

### Step 5: Warm them up — the ACA Framework
When they respond, use **Acknowledge → Compliment → Ask**:

- **Acknowledge:** Restate what they said in your own words. Shows you're listening.
  > "Two kids and you're running your own business…"
- **Compliment:** Connect to a positive trait.
  > "That's impressive — managing all that takes real discipline."
- **Ask:** Move the conversation toward your offer topic.
  > "Do you get much time to focus on growing the business?"

People love talking about themselves. Let them. When they feel good talking to you, trust and liking go up.

### Step 6: Make the offer — "Do you know anyone?"

After 3–4 exchanges, make the offer using the **value equation framing**:

> "By the way, do you know anyone who is [their struggle] and is looking to [dream outcome] in [timeframe]? I'm working with 5 people for free — I just want to get testimonials. I help them [outcome] without [effort/sacrifice]. I even guarantee [result] or I keep working with them. I just had [name] do this and they [specific result] even though they [same struggle]. Anyone come to mind?" *(pause)* "…and if no one comes to mind, does anyone you hate come to mind?" *(breaks tension)*

**Note:** Asking if they know someone (not if they want it) removes pressure. Most people who say they know someone are actually interested themselves.

**Short version for texts/DMs:**
> "I help [ideal customer] get [outcome] in [timeframe] without [effort] — [proof/guarantee]."

### Step 7: Make it easy to say yes — start free

> "I'm only taking 5 people so I can give everyone real attention. It's free as long as you: 1) actually use it, 2) give me honest feedback, and 3) leave a review if you think it deserves one. Does that sound fair?"

**Why offer free first:**
- You get reps without the pressure of failure
- People are forgiving when there's no charge
- Free customers convert, refer, and give testimonials — all three make you money

### Step 8: Start back at the top
After exhausting one platform, go to the second largest, then the third.

### Step 9: Start charging
When people start referring — start charging. Use the "every 5 rule":
- Free for first 5
- 80% off for next 5
- 60% off for next 5
- 40% off, etc.
- Keep raising 20% every 5 until close rates drop

### Step 10: Keep the list warm
Send regular value via email/social. Re-probe the list periodically with the **9-word email**:

> "Are you still looking to [4-word desire]?"
> Examples: "Are you still looking to lose 20 pounds?" / "Are you still looking to get more gym leads?"

No images, no links, just the question. People who reply are your hottest leads.

## When They Say No

"No" = feedback. Ask:

> "What would I have to do to make it worth your while?"

Their answer tells you how to improve your offer — and often gives you the hook to close the next person.

Yeses give opportunity. Nos give feedback. Either way, you win.

## Benchmarks

| Metric | Benchmark |
|---|---|
| Reply rate from warm list | ~20% (1 in 5) |
| Take free offer | ~20% of replies (1 in 5) |
| Convert free to paid | ~25% of free (1 in 4) |
| Net: paid customers per 100 reach-outs | ~1 |
| Revenue potential (100/day, $400 offer) | $104,000/year |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Mass blasting the list | Personal, one-at-a-time only — don't be a spam monster |
| Leading with the offer | Warm them up first (ACA framework) — 3–4 exchanges before the pitch |
| Asking if they want it | Ask if they know anyone — the no-pressure frame converts better |
| Stopping after 1–2 tries | Contact each person up to 3 times; timing matters |
| Waiting until list is "ready" | Start immediately; 100 reach-outs per day is the standard |
