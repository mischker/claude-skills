# GOATed Ads — Ad Production at Scale

## Overview

Most businesses hit a ceiling on ad spend and assume they've saturated the market. The real problem: they've run out of ad *variety*, not audience.

The solution: produce ads like an assembly line — separate hook, meat, and CTA into interchangeable parts, then combine them at scale.

**The math:**
- 50 hooks × 3–5 meats × 1–3 CTAs = **150–750 ads per week**
- This protects against ad fatigue, makes reverse-engineering hard for competitors, and finds winning combinations faster

---

## Why Ads Hit a Ceiling

Audiences exist on a spectrum from warmest to coldest:

1. **Most Aware** — knows your product, just needs the deal
2. **Product-Aware** — knows what you sell, unsure if it's right for them
3. **Solution-Aware** — wants the result but doesn't know your product
4. **Problem-Aware** — knows they have a problem but doesn't know solutions
5. **Completely Unaware** — doesn't even know they have a problem

When you start, your ads convert the warmest, smallest audiences. As you scale, you're reaching colder, larger audiences — and those require better, more varied creative. Hitting a wall at $5–10K/day means you've exhausted your current creative quality, not the market.

The fix: more ads, better matching of hook to awareness level.

---

## The Assembly-Line Process

Break ad creation into 3 parts. Make all of each part before moving to the next.

```
Hook (50) × Meat (3-5) × CTA (1-3) = 150–750 ads
```

### Part 1: Hooks (80% of prep time)

The hook determines everything. Spend most time here. See `hook-writing` skill for hook types.

**Sources for hooks:**
1. Your past winning ads (sorted by revenue generated, not views)
2. Your top organic content (tweet/caption → ad hook)
3. Winning hooks from competitors' ads
4. Winning hooks from other people's content in your niche
5. Ad libraries (last resort — hard to know what's actually working)

**Expansion hooks by awareness level** (for scaling to colder audiences):

| Awareness | Hook Style | B2C Weight Loss Example |
|---|---|---|
| Most Aware | Offer-driven | "XFast's new formula: Now with 25% more protein" |
| Product-Aware | Proof-driven | "Why 9/10 XFast users reached goal weight in 3 months" |
| Solution-Aware | Promise-driven | "Lose 15 pounds in 30 days with our proven system" |
| Problem-Aware | Pain-driven | "Frustrated with diets that don't last? There's a better way" |
| Completely Unaware | Curiosity-driven | "The hidden hormonal imbalance making 1 in 3 Americans gain weight" |

Winning hooks in one industry often work in another. Mine other niches.

### Part 2: Meat (20% of prep time)

The body of the ad. 3–5 well-crafted scripts. Each covers the What-Who-When value elements (see `paid-ads`). You don't need 50 versions — just 3–5 solid executions of your best angles.

Focus meat on:
- Dream outcome
- Contrast (what life looks like with/without your solution)
- Social proof (customer story or results)
- Mechanism (how you solve it differently)

### Part 3: CTA (minimal prep)

1–3 versions. Call to action should be:
- Specific: "Click the button below and book your free call"
- Easy: short URL, obvious next step
- Urgent: "I only have 5 spots this month" / "offer closes Friday"

CTAs don't drive performance much. Don't over-engineer them.

## Recording Session Setup

**Before recording:**
- Review your top hooks of all time (from your tracker)
- Write 40–50 hook variations; record ~10 per content piece
- Write 3–5 solid meats; record each cleanly
- Record 1–3 CTAs

**During recording:**
- Record every hook variation separately (not just your favorite)
- Keep meat recordings clean and re-usable (no mention of specific hook content)
- Keep CTAs generic enough to pair with any hook/meat combo

**After recording:**
- Edit in combinations (hook A + meat 1 + CTA 1 = ad; hook A + meat 2 + CTA 1 = another ad, etc.)
- Test all combinations; identify winner within one week
- Kill losers, scale the winner, retire it to your proven library

---

## Weekly Ad Production Rhythm

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Monday–Thursday | Prep: write 40–50 hooks, 3–5 meat scripts |
| Friday | Record everything (hooks, meats, CTAs) |
| Following Monday | Launch new test ads (1 test per platform rule) |
| Following Monday | Review results from prior week; log winners |

---

## Scaling Rules

1. **Test one thing at a time.** Multiple changes = can't learn what worked.
2. **Log every test.** Never start from scratch again.
3. **Kill losers fast.** If an ad spends 1x your target CAC with zero leads → kill it.
4. **Scale winners hard.** Once profitable, multiply budget quickly before it fatigues.
5. **80/20/10 on hooks.** 70% proven, 20% winner-adjacent, 10% experiments (see `hook-writing`).

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Making 5 ads/month | Make 150–750 ads/week with assembly-line approach |
| Spending equal time on hook and meat | 80% of prep on hooks — nothing else matters if hook fails |
| Testing multiple changes at once | One variable per test per platform, per week |
| Blaming market saturation | If you're under $10M/year, you haven't saturated anything |
| Not tracking hook performance | Build a spreadsheet. Your hook library is a business asset |
